# Mars

Mars is an authoritarian state controlling a vast network of subterranean cities. Modelled on surveillance states — secret police monitor all activity, and the state has a vested interest in every part of society. It was originally founded by a private enterprise and the language of the state is steeped in corporate speech, adapted into socialist ideology. The planet is run by the Board — representatives of the people — and the arms of government are all "departments."

Due to the cost of maintaining surface-level structures (radiation and storms), only the very wealthy ever see the sun. Society is rigidly stratified. Rovers and surface trains are luxury goods.

The map of Mars is like a tube map — all cities connected by highly controlled trains.

**Tone:** Grey, strong, stubborn, cold, utilitarian.

---

## Communities

**State Cities** — Brutalist, utilitarian, named things like Sector Iota-4 or Nexus Eleven. Almost completely underground, surrounded by engineering factories that pump smog into the atmosphere (the lower floors are sooty and smoky). Neon-lit corridors buzz with surveillance drones. Floors are strictly ranked:
- Upper tiers: civil servants, party loyalists, elevated citizens
- Mid tiers: workers, factory families, indoctrinated youth
- Lower tiers: migrants, dissidents, indentured criminals

**Breakaway Cities ("Craters")** — Hidden in abandoned mining shafts, lava tubes, or collapsed transport nodes. Jury-rigged lights, water reclamators, and illegally hacked train access. Often ruled by syndicates, rebel militias, or cults of personality. Some try to recreate democratic enclaves; others fall into warlordism. Surface scavengers and hackers build decoy pings to confuse State mapping. Life is hard, dirty, and free — mostly.

**Outposts** — Factories and mining rigs where the indentured harvest raw materials. Drilling in intense heat through inhospitable terrain. Entire families are born and die on-site. Whispers say some outposts aren't manned by humans anymore — just automated drones, repurposed construction bots, and something else.

**Surface Domes** — Very few surface communities exist. The richest 0.01% live in domes perched atop mountains or cliffs, where solar mirrors capture sunlight and redirect it into glimmering boulevards. Visiting the surface is a reward, an honor. Ordinary Martians live their entire lives without ever seeing the sky.

## The State

> "The State sees all. The State provides. The State remembers."

**Surveillance:** The metro lines are tightly controlled — your passcard, biometric signature, and surveillance dossier must align or you're rerouted to detention. Broadcasts are constant: ideology, State announcements, ads for rations and loyalty work.

**Technology:** The State AI was dismantled during the War of Obedience, but fragments exist in forbidden code used by rebels, or secretly embedded in metro firmware.

**Propaganda:** An extensive machine maintains the narrative that the state is infallible and its leadership is the planet's savior.

**The State's own justification:**
> "The democracies of the old world were susceptible to corruption — it was more important to tell people what they wanted to hear than do what they needed. We learned from those mistakes. Instead of our society being sold at auction every few years, we have stability. We are glass, we are honest, we are strong together."

## Factions & Groups

**The Enforcers of Purity** — Secret police with enhanced surveillance gear and authority to detain anyone "for recalibration."

**Train Priests** — Technicians who keep the MetroRail running. Highly respected, almost religious in devotion. Some smuggle rebels.

**Oathborn** — Ex-rebels forcibly reconditioned into propaganda icons. Some regain memories. Some were never brainwashed at all.

**The Red Echo** — A ghost transmission that interrupts official broadcasts. Said to carry the voice of Mars itself.

**The Titan Project** — Rumors say a weapon is being built in the deep shafts beneath the capital. For who, no one knows.

## Daily Life in the Breakaway Cities

No official economy, but everyone works or they don't eat.

- **Scavs** ride makeshift trains or trek in vacuum suits to old tunnels and derelict outposts. They bring back wire, tools, memory shards, anything sellable.
- **Patchers** are the mechanics — part-electrician, part-surgeon, part-artist. They keep everything running.
- **Whisper-runners** carry encoded messages between cities or to rebel cells. They memorize data fragments to avoid detection.
- **Farmers** tend deep-grow mushrooms, insect clutches, and UV-leaf greens in high-salinity hydroponic beds.

The city never really sleeps. Power is unreliable and safety's never guaranteed. A rotating Watch patrols for cave-ins, fires, or infiltrators.
