# Floaters

A patchwork of small, semi-autonomous communities living in spinners, drifting ships, and space stations. Some are highly advanced, self-sustaining marvels of engineering. Others are barely hanging on. The one unifying factor is constant movement and detachment from planetary systems.

Floaters are independent and not trusted. Hard to pin down. Seen by most planetary powers as undisciplined, chaotic, and untrustworthy — not because of anything inherently sinister, but because of their lifestyle.

**Tone:** Varied. Free, precarious, suspicious of outsiders, fiercely independent.

---

## Types of Floater Communities

**Spinners (Artificial Gravity Stations)** — The most self-sufficient. Large rotating ships or space stations that use centrifugal force to generate gravity. Tightly-knit societies that emphasise cooperation and self-reliance. Economies based on recycling, asteroid mining, and trade with passing ships. Often develop eccentric customs distinct from any planetary culture.

**Zero-G Adrift Communities** — The poorest and most vulnerable. Unable to afford artificial gravity, these communities live in zero-G, making daily life a constant struggle. Resources are sparse; leadership is often held by whoever is most resourceful or brutal. Black market dealings are common. A cultural tradition: **Gravity Tattoos** — ink derived from space debris, marking the transition to adulthood.

**Nomadic Floaters** — Travelers in the truest sense. Constantly moving, with no permanent destination. Some are renegades, outlaws, or refugees; others are wanderers seeking knowledge or a place to belong. They guard **Elder's Maps** — charts of the stars passed down from elder members, marking safe routes, pirate-infested zones, and hidden or abandoned places. Deeply prized.

---

## Reputation & Relations

- **Mars** sees the Floaters as a threat to order — too free-spirited, too unpredictable
- **Venus** may see them as a curiosity, something to be harnessed for trade or culture, but still kept at a distance
- Both look down on them

Without consistent supplies, Floater communities can be easily undermined by the major powers. Their independence is their greatest strength — they can always choose to move, adapt, or disappear.

## Notable Figures

**Tariq Azim** — A former Mars factory worker who escaped by stowing away on a drifting vessel. Now a member of a nomadic flotilla and a well-known organiser of rebellions against corporate interests, maintaining a network of smugglers.

**Eun Ha-Lee** — Lead engineer aboard one of the largest self-sustaining spinners. Has developed alternative energy sources that don't rely on planetary imports, turning her ship into a beacon of independence.

**Joaquín "The Albatross" Mendoza** — An infamous smuggler and information broker operating between Venus and the outer planets. Has connections with various factions and is rumoured to know the secret pathways to the most hidden stations.
