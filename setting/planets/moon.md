# The Moon

A smaller but inventive community that strongly believes in scavenging what it can from the ruins of the Earth. A technocracy — the closest thing to a hive city, with communities living in craters for shielding from magnetic rays.

**Tone:** Chaotic, innovative, dirty, oily, mechanical.

---

## Society

The Moon's society is built around **contracts**. Everything is transactional, everything must be part of a binding agreement. There is no traditional currency — instead, the Moon uses **fulfillment credits**: promissory notes that are only worth something if the issuer's reputation remains intact.

- Most trade is done via recorded obligations and barter of services, brokered through clan registrars
- Each clan keeps internal ledgers and manages its own scrip; cross-clan trade is handled through long-negotiated mutual contracts
- Defaulting on a contract is worse than death — it leads to blacklisting, exile, or legal servitude

Clans and individuals are rated by their **fulfillment index** — a metric of how reliably they meet obligations. A person with a low index can't find work, rent living space, or get medical aid. *Index assassination* is a real form of social attack.

Most people are uninvolved in politics.

---

## Economy

**Mining & Extraction** — The Moon is a major source of rare isotopes and regolith-derived materials. Clans control sectors of the subsurface.

**Legal & Contractual Arbitration** — Lunar contract law is a high-value export service. Luna's arbitrators are infamous for being brutally thorough and entirely neutral — if paid enough. Mars buys arbitration and legal enforcement muscle from Luna constantly.

**Data Sanctuaries** — While public AI is banned, the Moon hosts analog and air-gapped data vaults. Traders, factions, and even Martian governors store sensitive records in sealed domes, guarded by long-term contracts and oathbound Clausekeepers.

**Debt Bondage & Contract Labor** — A person can sell years of their labor to pay off a debt, becoming a "bound." These contracts are often sold, traded, or inherited. Outsiders find this horrifying; Lunars see it as fair and voluntary.

---

## External Relations

- **Mars** buys raw materials, arbitration, and legal enforcement muscle from Luna
- **Venus** trades in volatile goods and rumors but is considered untrustworthy; lunar clans often deal only through intermediaries
- **Space Stations** rely on lunar micro-manufacturing and specialists, especially for environmental controls
