# Venus

Venus is inhabited kilometres above the surface, away from the radioactive storms that cover the planet. People live in floating cities treated like ships, almost always led by a captain — nautical language permeates every part of society.

It was founded later than Mars by a collection of countries that individually didn't have the wealth to set up systems, so it is incredibly diverse. After the Wipe, the only cities left flying were those that didn't rely on AI, so a culture of written-down knowledge and oral tradition became prevalent. Computers are used but only when necessary and largely distrusted.

The cities vary from flying theatres to cobbled-together, vapour-drenched messes of rope and metal, to raiding barges that live off ransacking other cities.

**Tone:** Colourful, bright, loud, excessive, emotional.

---

## Structure: The Skyfaring League

Venus isn't ruled. It's steered.

Floating cities — called **Skyships** or **Driftports** — are led by **Captains**, but not through bloodline. Leadership is earned through deeds and ratified by the crew or the city's populace. Closer to pirate democracy than imperial nobility.

Cities run like ships: the Captain sets course, but must listen to their Quartermasters, Weathercasters, and the **Votive Assembly** — representatives from different guilds (artists, engineers, traders, etc).

Cities might be:
- **Drift-clans** — tightly bonded communities descended from shared Earth origins
- **Mutinied ships** — cities founded when a population split off mid-flight, stealing a portion of tech and air systems
- **Art barges** — nomadic, party-based vessels that trade performance and culture instead of traditional goods

Captains serve until they die, are voted out, or someone challenges them and wins a **Trial of Proof** — a trial can be a race, duel, scavenger hunt, or performance battle, whatever the ship deems fair.

## Technology

No magical antigrav. Venusian cities stay afloat via:
- Aerostatic lift (helium and hydrogen compartments)
- Solar updraft turbines
- Thermal buoyancy envelopes

Each city must constantly maintain its altitude, adjust to corrosive winds, and stay ahead of high-pressure downdrafts. Crews are always tuning ballast systems, sealing hull fabric, and recalibrating sensors. Everyone knows a bit of engineering; kids grow up understanding air chemistry before algebra.

## Culture

- **Clothing = resume.** Embroidered glyphs or reactive threads display achievements. The flashier your coat, the more respect.
- **Emotion is currency.** Stoicism is frowned upon. To be loud, proud, and real is to be trustworthy.
- **Storytelling and performance** are passed down through dance, masked theatre, and song.

Tradition is held in **The Drift** — an ever-evolving oral history shared across ships. It's not sacred, but to knowingly lie when contributing to The Drift is grounds for exile.

---

## Resources & Survival

Venus is rich in solar energy, but **hydrogen is life**. Without regular supply runs or trade, a city might lose altitude, be unable to steer, or explode if gas mixture maintenance fails.

**Gasrunners** — heavily modified skiff pilots who dip into the lower atmosphere or dock with supply stations at the edge of orbit to retrieve hydrogen. These runs are so dangerous they've become spiritual acts. Pilots paint their skiffs with blessings or memorials.
