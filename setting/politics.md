# Politics

## Mars: Authoritarian Control and Rebellion

Mars is a tightly controlled authoritarian state. The government maintains absolute power through surveillance, fear, and loyalty. The **Supreme Council** (or equivalent) sits at the top, choosing high-ranking individuals for their loyalty and ability to enforce the regime's will. Beneath them, Governors of each subterranean city rule with an iron fist.

Rebel movements — dubbed freedom fighters or terrorists depending on who you ask — operate in secret, with no central leadership. They rely on guerrilla warfare, sabotage, and subversive acts. Many operate as independent cells with little communication between them to avoid being compromised.

*See `/setting/factions/ash-network.md`.*

## Venus: Artistic and Economic Fluctuations

Venus is not a unified state but a collection of floating city-states, each with its own governance. Political authority is often tied to cultural leadership — artistic or cultural leaders can rally the populace. Leadership is highly contested; coups and challenges are not uncommon.

**Trade and resource conflicts** — With hydrogen scarce, there is intense competition to control mining rights and trade routes. Corporate interests often intervene, bribing leaders or sponsoring raiders.

**Internal struggles** — Power struggles within each city-state around resource allocation often lead to rebellions, especially in poorer districts.

## The Moon: The Economy of Obligation

The Moon doesn't have traditional politics — it has contract law. Clans govern themselves, and inter-clan disputes are resolved through arbitration. The only cooperative inter-clan institution is the central registry that maintains fulfillment indices.

Politically, the Moon exports neutrality. Lunar contract arbitrators are considered trustworthy precisely because they have no political allegiances — only contractual ones.

*See `/setting/factions/breakers-of-the-word.md` for the revolutionary opposition.*

---

## Floaters: Self-Governance and Survival

Floater political structure is self-governance with no overarching authority. People live by the laws of the ship or collective agreements. This results in either egalitarian systems where everyone has a say, or brutal hierarchies where those in power control everything.

Some floater communities have valuable relationships with larger planetary powers — trading in smuggled goods, offering reconnaissance for hire, or providing safe havens for those fleeing more authoritarian systems.

---

## Inter-Faction Tensions

**Mars vs. Venus** — Hostility rooted in fundamentally different systems. Mars sees Venus as decadent and unstable. Venus sees Mars as a prison-like militarised regime that stifles creativity. Diplomatic relations are strained; tensions spill over into trade wars and resource conflicts.

**Floaters vs. Everyone** — Floaters are looked down upon by both Mars and Venus. Their independence and resourcefulness make them potential allies or threats. Their lawless, unpredictable nature makes them dangerous in the eyes of established governments.

**The Moon as Neutral Ground** — Lunar arbitrators are used by all sides precisely because they have no political stakes beyond the contractual ones. This makes the Moon valuable but also constantly under pressure from all parties seeking to co-opt or corrupt its neutrality.

**The Black Echo as Pressure Valve** — All major powers permit the Black Echo a symbolic presence. Allowing them in is the cost of avoiding greater scrutiny. They are simultaneously resented and relied upon. See `/setting/factions/black-echo.md`.
