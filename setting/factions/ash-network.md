# The Ash Network

> "Freedom isn't a place, it's a current. You ride it quiet, or you drown."
> "We are what's left when the fire dies down. And we're still warm."

A loose confederation of rebels, smugglers, rogue engineers, ideologues, and opportunists operating against the Martian state. Not united by vision — just opposition.

---

## Internal Factions

**The Wyrms** — Ex-metro engineers who know how to tap into the ancient tunnels beneath the transit lines. They run secret trains and smuggle people, messages, and supplies.

**Red Daughters** — A radical cell focused on disrupting state surveillance. They hack terminals, sabotage mirror nodes, and upload fake identity strings to confuse tracking.

**The Grubs** — Scavengers from Breakaway Cities. They barter in parts, black-market medicine, and stolen IDs. Some only help for payment; others are truer believers than the radicals.

**The Smoke Saints** — A quasi-religious sect that believes the Red Order is a demonic machine. They graffitize sacred walls, bomb propaganda centers, and run reeducation "reverse rituals."

---

## How They Move

**Ghost Trains** — Short modified transit carts that piggyback onto the metro system, running right after official trains. Some rails contain obsolete maintenance spurs — dead-end tunnels sealed decades ago — that the Wyrms reopen.

**Improvised Tunnels** — Breakaway cities are often connected by ancient lava tubes. When stealth is needed, smugglers travel through ventilation shafts, plumbing arteries, or underground hydroponic waste systems.

**Papers & Identity** — Stolen, spliced, or fabricated bio-keys let rebels slip through stations undetected. Some smugglers run black-market memory implants, giving people new histories to fool State background checks.

---

## How They Fight

- **Signal floods** — Red Daughters jam the Mirror Network by blasting false citizen data, forcing temporary blackouts
- **Kinetic graffiti** — Patterns in UV-reactive ink that activate when scanned, triggering backdoor scripts or unlocking access panels
- **Decoy fires** — Small controlled arsons to distract Harmony Enforcers while another team hits a reactor node or pulls off a heist

Guns are rare — ammo is too hard to come by. Most carry electromagnetic shivs, repurposed mining lasers, or shock gloves pulled from factory defense units.

---

## Safe Havens & Signals

Hidden waystations in the metro lines serve as caches for gear, food, and messages. Marked only with rebel sigils — tiny burn-marks shaped like a flame or a crescent ash mark.

Public terminals are hijacked to play a certain rhythm of tones — a "whistlecode" telling rebels when it's safe to meet.

**Ash Gardens** — Secret growrooms of food and medicine hidden beneath old transit maintenance sheds. Rumoured to include one that moves itself through the tunnels.

---

## Risks

- **The Harmony Algorithm** — The state's predictive policing system. A rebel seen in more than one sector in a week gets flagged.
- **Echo Recalibration** — If captured, rebels may be mindwiped and reprogrammed into sleeper agents. No one can ever fully trust a returned comrade.
- **Betrayal** — Infiltrators from the Order sometimes pose as radicals to destroy cells from within.

---

## Legendary Figures

**The Conductor** — An ex-Transit Authority programmer who controls at least two ghost trains. Never seen. Said to have a map of every tunnel on Mars.

**"Mother Cinder"** — Elder rebel who maintains a school for Ash children — orphans and runaways — deep in the vents. Teaches survival, sabotage, and storytelling.

**Noct** — A masked saboteur who only strikes during solar flares, when comms are down. Believed to be multiple people using the same identity.
