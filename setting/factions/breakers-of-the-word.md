# The Breakers of the Word

> "To bind the soul is to imprison it. To break the contract is to free the spirit from the machine."

The Breakers believe the Moon's obsession with contract and order is the true form of slavery. To them, the sanctity of the contract — the sacred, binding clause — is a tool of the authoritarian elites to control the masses. Their goal is to destroy the system that upholds it.

---

## Core Beliefs

**The Contract is the Cage** — Contracts force people into roles, define their limits, and trap them in an unending cycle of servitude.

**The Word is False** — Words can be twisted, altered, erased. Breaking a contract means reclaiming true, self-defined power.

**Liberation through Destruction** — The path to enlightenment is not adherence to contracts, but their destruction.

**The Cult of the Void** — They worship the idea of the Void: the space outside the system where contracts do not exist. Absolute freedom, unbound by laws or society.

**The Final Break** — They believe the Moon itself must be unmade. The original contract that binds the domes together should be ripped apart. Some see this as literal destruction; others as a metaphysical event — the collapse of the contract-based society.

---

## Practices

**Ritual Burnings** — Members gather to burn their personal contracts, chanting the clauses they are rejecting.

**Nullification Ceremonies** — Secret ceremonies where they declare null and void the most sacred clauses of society, rewriting them with alternative language.

**Contract Breaking** — The ultimate form of worship is breaking a major contract — whether personal, familial, or clan-level. Seen as spiritual liberation, though it often results in brutal retaliation.

**No Signatures** — Rituals that refuse to sign any contract, no matter the personal cost.

**Eternal Wandering** — Self-imposed exile to escape all contracts, carrying nothing. A form of asceticism; breaking all ties with society as a final step toward transcendence.

---

## Position in Lunar Society

Hunted and disregarded by most of the legal system, viewed as radical, anarchic, and dangerous.

Some lunar elites secretly fund them — seeing their actions as useful to divide the lower classes and keep them distracted, but never fully free.

Exiled members who have broken their contracts are sometimes treated as martyrs by the lower-class contractless, who see them as freedom fighters.

---

## Underground Networks

They operate among the contractless, hacking the legal system to erase or forge documents, and spreading propaganda to inspire revolution.

**Shadow Cities** — Hidden in tunnels beneath major domes, the Breakers maintain safe houses: abandoned service hubs, decommissioned stations, mines that once housed resource workers.

**Minds of the Ink** — Some Breakers have infiltrated or trained as Clausekeepers, understanding contract law from the inside. They appear as legitimate legal officers while working toward its destruction.
