# Drones

Drones are small, animal-shaped remote units used by Mech Controllers to act at a distance. A drone is an item — it occupies Load in your inventory, is deployed and stored at will, and has its own item card with tags and modifications.

The **Mech Controller** specialism is required to operate a drone. See `mechanics/perks.md`.

By default, a Mech Controller operates one drone at a time. Perks (Split Focus, Hive Mind) extend this.

---

## Deploying and Storing

- Deploying or storing a drone takes an action.
- While a drone is deployed, the pilot acts *through* it — not directly. You cannot do both simultaneously.
- If you store the drone or it goes offline, you act normally again.

---

## Acting Through a Drone

When the drone is deployed, treat it as your position in the fiction. You use your own skills and abilities, but outcomes are filtered through the drone's capabilities.

- If the drone has no weapons, you cannot make attacks through it.
- If the drone lacks the relevant tool or tag, actions that require it are not possible.
- The GM sets position and effect based on what the drone can physically do, not what you could do in person.

---

## Range

By default, a drone must remain within **line of sight** of the pilot.

Perks can extend this range.

---

## Wear

Drones have a wear track. Mark wear when the drone takes damage or is pushed beyond its limits.

- When all wear is marked, the drone **jams** — it goes offline and cannot be used until repaired.
- A jammed drone is not destroyed. It can be repaired during downtime or in the field with the right tools.

---

## Drone Item Card

A drone is an item card like any other. Its default profile:

| Item | Load | Wear |
|---|---|---|
| Drone | 2 | 3 |

A drone's capabilities come entirely from its **tags and modifications**. A drone with no tags can observe and move, nothing more.

Example tags a drone might carry:
- *Armed* — carries a weapon; specify which one
- *Compact* — fits through small spaces a person couldn't
- *Silent* — movement makes no detectable noise
- *Reinforced* — +1 wear
- *Long-range transmitter* — extends pilot range beyond line of sight

Tags and modifications follow the same rules as any other item (see `mechanics/equipment.md`).
