# Advancement

Characters advance by spending XP on new perks or higher action ratings.

---

## Earning XP

XP comes from three sources.

### 1. Failure

When any roll results in **1–3**, mark 1 XP. This applies to all rolls: action rolls, resistance rolls, assist rolls, Devil's Bargains.

This is the primary driver of advancement. Characters with lower action ratings fail more often and advance faster in those areas — the rate naturally slows as ratings improve.

### 2. Specialism Trigger (in play)

Each Specialism has a trigger that fires in the moment — not at end of session. When you do your defining method in a way that creates an opening or consequence for the crew, mark XP immediately.

> Specialism XP triggers are not yet written. Each should follow the pattern: doing your method in a way that creates a tangible opening or consequence for the crew — not just using the skill, but using it so it matters.

### 3. End of Session

At the end of every session, run through the following questions.

**Personal (each player answers for themselves):**
- Did you demonstrate your background, or struggle against it? If yes, mark XP.
- Did your relationship with a crewmate, NPC, or faction meaningfully shift? If yes, mark XP.

The first question points at the behavioral drive you authored in Step 1 of character creation — the statement that describes how your past tends to shape your behavior. XP comes from either leaning into it or fighting it. Both count.

**Group (everyone marks XP for each "yes"):**
- Did the crew complete or meaningfully advance an objective?
- Did the crew take on a threat bigger than themselves?
- Did the crew's reputation change — with anyone?

Maximum per session: 2 personal XP + up to 3 group XP.

---

## Spending XP

XP can be spent at any time — there is no downtime gate.

| Advancement | Cost |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 perk | 6 XP |
| Tier 2 perk | 12 XP |
| Tier 3 perk | 18 XP |
| Action rating +1 | 6 XP |

You must meet any prerequisites listed on a perk before purchasing it.

Action ratings cap at **2**. An action rating already at 2 cannot be upgraded further.

> Open question: Is there an XP discount for perks within your Specialism? See `mechanics/perks.md`.

## Crew XP (Optional)

Crew XP is not yet decided. If included, it will award a maximum of **2 XP per session** to the whole crew based on crew-type behaviour. See `core-mechanics.md` for crew type definitions.
