# Weapon Tags

Tags are passive properties on weapons. They describe what a weapon can do beyond its base stats. Some tags carry mechanical effects; others are primarily fictional cues.

---

## Automatic
Spend X ammo to add X damage and X targets to a shot (there must be X targets).

---

## Cleave
Add another target next to you.

---

## Concealable
Easy to hide amongst regular clothing.

---

## Consumable
Single use. Once used, the weapon is gone.

---

## Counterweighted
Adds a die when being used to resist an attack in melee.

---

## Electrified
Can be used to overload electric systems, or shock an enemy.

---

## Explosive
Hits everyone nearby to the target.

---

## Knock Back
Knock an enemy back a few paces or down.

---

## Magnetic
Has a powerful magnet that can be activated.

---

## Multi-tool
Has other small tools attached, so can be used for a variety of purposes.

---

## Non-lethal
You knock an enemy unconscious rather than kill them outright.

---

## Parry
Spend a wear to roll an action to resist damage from a melee attack.

---

## Pull
Pull the enemy closer towards you.

---

## Scoped
−1 to rolls when you haven't spent time lining up the shot, or against fast-moving opponents. +1 when you do.

---

## Small Blade
−1d when fighting in melee against enemies with bigger weapons than yours. +1d against an enemy you have grappled.

---

## Spread
Add an extra target next to the original.

---

## Tethered
Has a zip cable attached so it can be quickly pulled back towards you.

---

## Throw-balanced
Can be thrown one range further than specified, or thrown to Nearby range if none is specified.

---

## Throwable
Can be thrown. See Throw-balanced for extended range options.
