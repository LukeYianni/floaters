# Clocks

## Purpose

Clocks track progress — toward goals, toward completion, toward consequences. They apply the same mechanic to any situation where something builds over time rather than resolving in a single roll.

---

## What Is a Clock

A clock is a circle divided into segments. Ticks fill segments. When all segments are filled, the thing the clock represents happens.

The fiction determines what the clock means. The mechanic is the same regardless:
- A crew working toward a long-term project
- A wound healing over downtime
- Antagonists mobilising a response
- A situation escalating toward a point of no return

---

## Clock Size

The GM sets the number of segments based on scope and difficulty. Larger clocks represent harder or longer tasks; smaller clocks resolve faster.

The healing clock is a fixed exception: always 4 segments (see `mechanics/wounds.md`).

---

## Ticking a Clock

Ticks are added based on the roll result and the effect level of the action.

| Roll | Limited | Standard | Great |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 1–3 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| 4–5 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| 6 | 2 | 3 | 5 |
| Crit | 3 | 5 | 7 |

Standard effect is the default. Effect level is set by the GM based on fictional positioning (see `mechanics/rolling.md`).

Clocks can be ticked at any point — during a session or between them. There is no strict boundary between mission and downtime for clock progression.

---

## When a Clock Fills

The thing the clock represents happens. No additional roll is required.

- A goal clock fills → the goal is achieved
- A threat clock fills → the threat lands
- A healing clock fills → wounds improve (see `mechanics/wounds.md`)
- A project clock fills → the project is complete

The GM narrates the outcome. Fiction determines consequences and follow-on events.

---

## Clocks Going Backward

Ticks can be removed. This is rare — it represents a meaningful setback, not a routine outcome.

The GM removes ticks as a consequence of failure, opposition, or a significant fictional reversal. Players do not remove ticks from threat clocks through normal rolls; doing so requires a fictional justification and a relevant action.

---

## Clock Ownership

- **Characters** — personal clocks such as healing and advancement
- **Crew** — shared project or goal clocks
- **Antagonists** — threat or mobilisation clocks run by the GM
- **Situations** — escalation clocks tied to a location or event, owned by no one

The GM tracks antagonist and situation clocks. Players track their own.
