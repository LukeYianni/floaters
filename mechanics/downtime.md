# Downtime

## Overview

You can take as many downtime actions as you want. Every time you take a downtime action, all antagonists also take a downtime action. Keep clocks running for NPCs and roll dice for them during their actions.

---

## How to Take a Downtime Action

1. Choose a downtime activity.
2. Roll an appropriate action (see `rolling.md`).
3. Apply ticks to the relevant clock based on your result.

---

## Effect and Ticks

By default, downtime actions have **limited effect**. Ticks per result:

| Roll | Limited | Standard | Great |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 1–3  | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| 4–5  | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| 6    | 2 | 3 | 5 |
| Crit | 3 | 5 | 7 |

---

## Increasing Effect

Bring additional resources to upgrade from limited to standard effect:

- **Tool** — mark wear on the tool
- **Crew member** — use crew dice for that character
- **Room** — no wear marked (one of the main benefits of having a room)

---

## Downtime Activities

### Healing

Recover from injuries. Roll and apply ticks to your healing clock.

Each character has a personal **healing clock** with 4 segments. When the clock fills:
- All wounds shift down one tier (Critical → Major, Major → Minor)
- Minor wounds clear
- Reset the clock to 0 and begin again if wounds remain

A character with only Minor wounds recovers in one completion. A Critical wound requires three.

### Repairing

Fix damaged gear or vehicles. Roll and apply ticks to the repair clock for the item.

### Training

Advance a skill or attribute. Roll and apply ticks to an advancement clock.

### Long-Term Project

Work toward a larger goal tracked by a clock. Roll and apply ticks to the project clock. Define the clock and its scope when the project begins.
