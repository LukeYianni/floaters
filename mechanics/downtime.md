# Downtime

## Overview

You can take as many downtime actions as you want. Every time you take a downtime action, all antagonists also take a downtime action. Keep clocks running for NPCs and roll dice for them during their actions.

---

## Effect Scale

Downtime actions use a standard roll result table:

| Roll | Ticks |
|------|-------|
| 1–3  | 1 tick |
| 4–5  | 2 ticks |
| 6    | 3 ticks |
| Crit | 5 ticks |

By default, downtime actions have **limited effect** — everything shifts one level back:

| Roll | Ticks (limited effect) |
|------|------------------------|
| 1–3  | 0 ticks |
| 4–5  | 1 tick |
| 6    | 2 ticks |
| Crit | 3 ticks |

---

## Increasing Effect

You can upgrade from limited to standard effect by bringing additional resources:

- **Tool** — mark wear on the tool
- **Crew member** — use crew dice for that character
- **Room** — no wear marked (one of the main benefits of having a room)

---

## Downtime Activities

### Healing

Recover from injuries. Roll and apply ticks to your healing clock.

### Repairing

Fix damaged gear or vehicles. Roll and apply ticks to the repair clock for the item.

### Training

Advance a skill or attribute. Roll and apply ticks to an advancement clock.

### Long-Term Project

Work toward a larger goal tracked by a clock. Roll and apply ticks to the project clock. Define the clock and its scope when the project begins.
