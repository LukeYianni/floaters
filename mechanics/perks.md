# Perks

Perks are character abilities organised into **Domains** — thematic skill trees covering different facets of gameplay. Each character chooses one Domain as their **Specialism**, gaining its starting ability and equipment at character creation.

> Open question: Can characters purchase perks from outside their Specialism? Is there an XP discount for perks within your Specialism?

---

## Characteristics

Each perk is associated with one of three Characteristics:

- **Deft** — precision, speed, finesse
- **Guile** — cunning, knowledge, deception
- **Grit** — toughness, resolve, endurance

---

## Tiers

Perks are ranked by Tier, representing power and advancement cost:

- **Starter** — available at character creation
- **Tier 1** — early advancement
- **Tier 2** — mid advancement
- **Tier 3** — late advancement

---

## Specialisms

A Specialism is a character's chosen Domain. It grants a starting ability and starting items.

| Specialism | Starting Ability | Starting Items |
|---|---|---|
| **Biomechanics** | Stabiliser — You can heal team members mid-battle. The injury still persists but the negative modifier is from the tier below. | Implant and field surgeon kit |
| **Enduring** | You ignore the first instance of melee damage in a fight. It does not cost you stress to get in the way of an attack made against an ally next to you. | Armour and a melee weapon (with mod, TBD) |
| **Explosives** | *(not yet defined)* | Demolitionist tools and grenades |
| **Hacking** | *(not yet defined)* | Hacking tools and EMP |
| **Killing** | When you succeed in using a weapon, choose 3 options from the weapon abilities list (5 on a crit). | A weapon with some mods |
| **Mech Controller** | You have the natural ability to pick up the controls of any mech you connect to. | A mech |
| **Movement** | *(not yet defined)* | Mag-boots, a short-burst jetpack, and a stabiliser harness |
| **Salvager** | Once per session you can change a negative tag on a piece of loot to a positive one. | Salvaging tools and a basic carrying drone |
| **Stealth** | *(not yet defined)* | Lockpicks and infrared goggles |
| **Tracker** | When travelling to a place you've previously visited, you can identify the shortest, most direct path. Any rolls led by you required to get there have +1d. | Multispectrum visor, environmental scanner, and a set of tag darts |

> Open question (Killing specialism): Should you always choose one extra option on any hit, with crits granting even more?

## Perk List

### Assassination

**Ambusher** — Tier 1, Deft
- +1d to attacks against a target who is unaware of you.

**Anything sharp** — Tier 2, Grit
- You can pick up any small item and use it as a standard weapon with damage 1.

**Chink in the armour** — Tier 2, Guile
- If you've observed a target for a few moments without them seeing you as a threat, your first attack against them ignores all armour.

**Poisoner** — Tier 2, Guile
- You know how to make poisons and can use a downtime action to do so.
- *(Open: poison types and effects list not yet designed.)*

### Ballistics

**Killer** — Tier Starter
- You get to choose one extra option whenever you fire a weapon from the list of options you have.

**Ammo hoarder** — Tier 1, Grit
- You have ammo crammed into every pocket. Your guns have an extra 3 ammo.

**Gun nut** — Tier 1, Guile
- You know how to modify guns, and learn three modifications of your choice.

**Targeting body parts** — Tier 1, Deft
- You can cut 1 to target a limb.

**Boom Headshot** — Tier 2, Guile
- When you successfully kill a target that you have aimed down a sight to hit, you still gain the scoped benefits until you either miss or are attacked.

**Gun nut - 2** — Tier 2
- You can add modifiers to guns.
- *(Open: balance mechanism not yet designed.)*

### Biomechanics

**Robotic Leg** — Tier 1, Grit
- You can replace a leg with an electronic equivalent. When created, choose one:
  - That leg is supernaturally strong.
  - The leg has a small jet that can propel you short distances.
  - The leg is hollow and can store up to 1 load inside it (counts as hidden).

**Robotic Arm** — Tier 1, Grit
- You can replace an arm with an electronic equivalent. When created, choose one:
  - That arm is supernaturally strong.
  - The fingers are sharp to a blade point.
  - Your arm can emit a shock on touch.
  - Your fingerprints can mimic someone else's.

**Photographic Eyes** — Tier 1, Deft
- You can take photos with your eyes, saved to a small memory device inside your head. In addition you can augment your lenses to look great distances with clarity.

**Neural Link** — Tier 2, Guile
- You can communicate via thought to other members using the same connected neural link.

**Mental Malware** — Tier 3, Grit
- You have modified your neural implants to network-attack a connected link. You can attempt to override the neural implants in an enemy's brain, controlling their action for a brief period of time while they try to wrest control back from you.

**Neural DDoS** — Tier 3, Guile
- You can push yourself and mark wear to your neural link to try to overwhelm a nearby neural link. This can incapacitate at the least and cause a brain haemorrhage at the worst.

**Subdermal Plating** — Tier 3, Grit
- You have learnt how to implant a thin metal mesh under the skin. You get two extra armour usages.

**EM eyes** — Tier 3, Deft
- You can modify a character's eyes to see another part of the physical light spectrum.

### Leadership

**Leader** — Tier Starter, Grit
- You can push yourself after an ally rolls so they can reroll their dice. They must take the new result.
- *(Open: Can they reroll all their dice, or only roll one additional die?)*

**Cut the crap** — Tier 1, Grit
- You are immune to the effects of an ally's ticks.

**Effective Assistance** — Tier 1, Deft
- When you assist an ally, you can take 2 stress to give them both +1d and increased effect instead.

**Good listener** — Tier 1
- Push yourself to use another character's home or background.

**Got your back** — Tier 1, Guile
- When you assist an ally, it costs them only 1 stress to push themselves instead of two.

**Shared intel** — Tier 1, Guile
- When you gather intelligence, hold 1 forward for when someone acts on that intel.

**Good luck charm** — Tier 2
- +1 to fortune rolls.

**Helping hand** — Tier 2, Grit
- When an ally resists, you can take up to half of the resulting stress in their place.

**Inspirational** — Tier 2, Grit
- As a downtime action, you can console and motivate your team. Roll Grit and distribute that many points among teammates to clear strain.

**Inspiring Criticals** — Tier 2, Deft
- Once per session when you critically succeed, all allies who can see you clear a stress.

**Lead from the front** — Tier 2, Grit
- When you take a wound, the next ally who acts gets +1d.

**Overclocker** — Tier 1, Grit

**Spare Parts** — Tier 1, Grit

**Hive Mind** — Tier 3, Guile

**Split focus** — Tier 3, Deft

**Charger** — Tier 1, Grit
- You get +1d to resist effects as a result of you running towards an enemy.

**It was a feint!** — Tier 2, Deft

**Parry** — Tier 2, Deft
- When using your weapon to resist the stress caused by a melee attack, you can use your Kill skill.

**Weighted Strike** — Tier 2, Grit

**No G? No Problem** — Tier 1, Deft

**Slipstream Rider** — Tier 1, Deft

**Sure-step** — Tier 1, Deft

**Aerodynamics Expert** — Tier 2, Deft

- *(Design note: under review — may be too passive/plain as written.)*

### Salvage

**It's a feature!** — Tier Starter, Guile
- You can mark wear on a person to remove a negative trait on their item during a scenario.

**Fast Cutdown** — Tier 1, Deft
- Mark wear to your tools to disassemble a large item into small usable parts quickly.

**Gold thumb** — Tier 1, Guile
- Once per session, you can turn a negative tag into a positive one for a resource you find.

**Makeshift Armour** — Tier 1, Grit
- Sacrifice an item to gain a single-use armour for the rest of the session.

**Needles in a Haystack** — Tier 1, Grit
- As a downtime action, sift through scrap and roll a d6: on a 1 find a piece of tech, 2 find ammo, 3 find fuel. You describe what it is as long as it makes sense. This uses the scrap.

**Fixer Upper** — Tier 2, Guile
- You can spend time and salvage to remove negative tags from ship parts and items.

**Jury-rigger** — Tier 2, Guile
- Push yourself to assemble scrap and debris into a relevant tool. The tool breaks down into unrecoverable debris after the session.

**Salvager** — Tier 2, Deft
- When you destroy a ship, roll to salvage an undestroyed part of it.

**Tech Salvager** — Tier 2, Guile
- Spend time to turn 10 scrap into 1 tech.

**Fixer Upper - 2** — Tier 3, Guile
- You can spend time modifying ship parts to add positive tags.
- *(Note: "Fixer Upper - 2" is a placeholder name.)*
