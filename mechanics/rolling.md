# Rolling

## The Dice Pool

When a character acts and the outcome is uncertain, they roll a pool of d6s. The pool is built from their rating in the relevant **action** (0–4 dice). Take the **highest single die** as the result.

The nine actions, grouped by characteristic:

| GRIT | DEFT | GUILE |
|------|------|-------|
| Fight | Finesse | Hack |
| Break | Hunt | Study |
| Fix | Prowl | Sway |

If your action rating is 0, roll two dice and take the **lowest**.

---

## Adding Dice

You can add dice to your pool before rolling:

- **Push Yourself** — spend 1 Edge to add 1d
- **Devil's Bargain** — add 1d, accept a consequence the GM names before you roll
- **Assist** — a teammate adds 1d and suffers consequences alongside you if the roll fails

---

## Reading the Result

| Roll | Outcome |
|------|---------|
| 1–3  | Failure — you don't do it, and things get worse |
| 4–5  | Partial success — you do it, but with a cost or compromise |
| 6    | Full success — you do it |
| Crit | Exceptional success — you do it with added benefit (multiple 6s) |

---

## Position

Position reflects how much danger or consequences are at stake if the roll goes badly. The GM sets position intuitively — it is not a named step in the conversation.

Position doesn't change the result table, but it shapes what a failure or partial success means: a 1–3 at desperate position is catastrophic; at controlled it's a minor setback.

---

## Effect

Effect reflects how much impact a success actually has. Default effect for most rolls is **standard**. The GM may call for **limited** effect when the approach is weak, or grant **great** effect when conditions are especially favorable.

See individual subsystem files for how effect interacts with specific mechanics (e.g. downtime clocks, harm).

---

## Resistance

When you suffer a consequence, you can attempt to resist it. Spend 1 Edge and roll a relevant action.

| Roll | Outcome |
|------|---------|
| 1–3  | Take the full consequence |
| 4–5  | Reduce the consequence by one tier |
| 6    | Avoid the consequence entirely |
| Crit | Avoid the consequence and turn it to your advantage |
