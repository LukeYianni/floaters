# Enemies

Anyone the crew might end up fighting. Includes people, robots, and anything else with intent and the capacity to hurt them.

---

## Format

**NAME**
*tags*

**Resilience**: X wounds
**Armor**: X
**Equipment**: what they're carrying

**Instinct**: to [do something]

**Moves:**
- ...

**Description**: brief, sensory, how to portray them at the table

---

## Tags

Tags describe how an enemy hunts, fights, and behaves. Choose all that apply.

### Organization (pick 1)
| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *solitary* | Acts alone |
| *pair* | Fights in twos, covers each other |
| *squad* | 3–6, trained to operate together |
| *horde* | Many; treat as a single entity or wave |

### Disposition (add all that apply)
| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *trained* | Disciplined; uses cover, flanks, doesn't panic |
| *armed* | Carries a meaningful weapon |
| *armored* | Wears protection (reflected in Armor stat) |
| *desperate* | Unpredictable; takes risks, may escalate beyond reason |
| *cautious* | Avoids direct confrontation; probes before committing |
| *ruthless* | No hesitation; goes for the most damaging option |
| *reckless* | Charges in; doesn't account for consequences |
| *organized* | Coordinates with others; calls for backup, uses signals |

### Special (add if relevant)
| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *augmented* | Cybernetic or biological enhancement that affects combat |
| *drone-linked* | Can direct or act through drones |
| *vacuum-capable* | Operates in hard vacuum without impairment |
| *constructed* | Not biological; immune to poison, suffocation, fear |

## Resilience

How much damage an enemy can take before they're incapacitated. Damage from weapons is a number (see `mechanics/weapons.md`); subtract it from resilience each hit.

| Resilience | Who |
|---|---|
| 1 | Mook; civilian caught in trouble, untrained thug |
| 2–3 | A real fight; trained gang member, enforcer |
| 4–6 | Tough; lieutenant, veteran soldier, hardened criminal |
| 7+ | Boss-tier; elite opposition, someone who's done this a long time |

Enemies at 0 resilience are incapacitated — unconscious, bleeding out, or surrendering. Whether they die is a fictional call, not a mechanical one.

---

## Armor

Armor reduces incoming damage by its rating. Subtract armor from the damage dealt before applying it to resilience.

Weapon damage runs 1–3. Armor ratings should sit in the same range to be meaningful.

| Armor | Effect | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No protection | Civilian clothes |
| 1 | Reduces all incoming damage by 1 | Light vest, heavy jacket |
| 2 | Reduces all incoming damage by 2 | Tactical vest, security gear |
| 3 | Reduces all incoming damage by 3 | Military plate, hardened suit |

Armor 1 means a pistol (1 damage) does nothing — you need a heavier weapon or a better position. Armor 3 means only the heaviest weapons do any damage at all.

Armor doesn't make enemies invincible — it changes what approach the crew needs to take. A well-armored enemy is a positioning problem, not just a damage problem.

## Equipment and moves

An enemy's gear determines what they can do. When you write up an enemy, list their notable equipment — then write moves that flow from it. Players can often see what someone is carrying. That's a promise about what's coming.

Write 3–5 moves total. Most should follow directly from equipment. Add 1–2 behavioral moves that reflect the instinct and tactics.

**When writing equipment moves:**
- Be specific about the effect ("convulsing, acts at disadvantage" not just "stunned")
- Let the move do something distinct — don't just describe a hit
- Think about what the item does *beyond* dealing wounds

---

## Instinct

Write as "to [verb]." A general pattern of behavior, not a specific action. Use it when you're unsure what the enemy does next.

- Good: "to eliminate witnesses", "to protect the operation", "to not be the one who takes the fall"
- Too specific (that's a move): "to shoot the nearest PC"
- Too vague: "to fight"

---

## Running enemies at the table

- **Let things burn.** Don't protect your enemies. If the fiction and the dice point toward a bad outcome for them, go with it.
- **Portray them as people.** Every enemy has an instinct and self-preservation. Many can be scared off, bribed, or reasoned with — especially mooks.
- **Telegraph what they're carrying.** If an enemy has a stun baton, describe it before it matters. That's fair warning and it builds tension.
- **Use the instinct.** When you don't know what an enemy does next, go with the instinct. It's always right.
- **Mooks go down fast.** Don't let a 1-wound enemy take up more table time than they're worth. One good hit, they're done.
