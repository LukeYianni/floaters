# Character Creation

## Purpose

Character creation is structured around three questions. Each question produces fictional grounding and mechanical outputs. Answer them in order.

---

## Step 1 — What Was Home?

Choose a **home** (a place) and a **background** (what you did there).

- Assign **+1 action rating** to one action that reflects your home.
- Assign **+1 action rating** to one action that reflects your background.

**Name one person you'd go back for.** Give them a name and a one-line description — who they are and what they mean to you. Mark them △.

> *Example: Sora, my sister — she ran the refinery accounts and covered for me more than once.*

This person is a slow-burn GM resource. They may appear in play when the crew is in the right place, or surface as a message, a rumour, or a name on a manifest. No XP is attached — they are story material.

---

## Step 2 — Why Did You Leave?

Choose an **entanglement** — something portable that follows you through the solar system regardless of where you are. This signals what kind of story your character is in and gives the GM material to tie you into wider arcs.

### Entanglement categories

- **Debt** — you owe something to a faction or person with reach, and they haven't forgotten
- **Wanted** — there's a warrant, a bounty, or someone actively looking for you
- **Obligation** — a promise, contract, or loyalty you took on and haven't fulfilled
- **Knowledge** — you're carrying something dangerous that someone wants
- **Reputation** — something you did that precedes your name wherever you go

Optionally, name a specific person associated with your entanglement. This fleshes out the world and gives the GM a face to put on it.

> *Example: Debt — I owe the Union three months of contracted runs I never delivered. Or: Debt — I owe Desta, a Union enforcer who let me walk once and is keeping score.*

> **Note for future development:** entanglement options should be expanded with faction-specific variants once the setting is more developed. Owing the Martian state is a different experience to owing the lunar colonies.

---

## Step 3 — What Is Your New Home?

### Specialism

Choose a **Specialism** (see `mechanics/perks.md`). This grants:

- A starting ability
- Starting equipment

### Action Ratings

Distribute **4 additional points** across the nine actions freely.

- Maximum **2 points** in any single action at creation.
- Combined with the 2 points from Step 1, no action may exceed 2 at the end of creation.

### Crew Bonds

Go around the table. Each player states one thing their character knows or believes about one other character. It doesn't have to be confirmed true.

### Ship Creation

As a group, build the crew's ship together. (Ship creation procedure: see ship system, not yet written.)

## Action Rating Summary

| Source | Points |
|---|---|
| Home (Step 1) | 1 |
| Background (Step 1) | 1 |
| Free distribution (Step 3) | 4 |
| **Total** | **6** |

All actions start at 0. Any action not assigned a point remains at 0 (roll two dice, take lowest).
