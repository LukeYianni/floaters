# Port Vala

An agricultural processing station run by MAPO — the Martian Agricultural Production Organisation. Raw produce comes in, gets packaged, goes out across the system. Most workers are temporary, cycling through on short contracts. Nobody really lives here; everyone's just passing through.

The tension: Mars controls this place from a distance, and that distance is exactly what lets things rot in the corners.

---

## Factions

**MAPO Administration**
- *Want:* Smooth operations. No scandals that reach Mars.
- *Move:* When something threatens to become visible, bury it in paperwork and reassignment.

**Martian Workforce**
- *Want:* Maintain their status above the foreign workers. Keep what they have.
- *Move:* When questioned by outsiders, close ranks and refer everything up the chain.

**Foreign Workforce**
- *Want:* The pay they're owed and conditions that don't kill them.
- *Move:* When pushed too far, talk to anyone who'll listen — including people they shouldn't trust.

**Martian Worker's Union**
- *Want:* Weaken MAPO's hold on the Engineering Department and expand their influence.
- *Move:* When they see a chance to embarrass the administration, take it regardless of collateral. When the Bears arrangement is threatened, warn them quietly rather than act openly.
- *Note:* The Union has a quiet arrangement with the Bears. The Bears' operation generates instability and resentment that the Union points to as evidence MAPO can't keep order. The Union gets leverage; the Bears get left alone by the one faction that could expose them to the workforce. Neither side trusts the other. Both find it useful. The Bears listen in on Union meetings regardless — that's just how the Bear operates.

**The Bears**
- *Want:* Keep the operation running. Keep Mars off their back. Keep the Cult supplied.
- *Move:* When a problem appears, find the angle that makes it disappear without a fight.

**The Cult of the Shepherd**
- *Want:* Information, growth, protection of the Shepherd.
- *Move:* When someone gets too close, offer the drug first — always framed as a friendly gesture.

## Key Locations

### The Burnt Market
Char marks still on the walls from the fire that gutted it years ago. The administration wrote it off; squatters moved in. The Bears run a rave here — loud, dark, and busy enough that no one looks too closely at anything. The bass is constant. You feel it before you hear it.

*What's happening here:* The rave runs every night as cover. Tonight is also a shipment night.

*GM moves:*
- When players ask around, someone sells them something useful — and immediately tells the Bear's people they were asking.
- When players are visibly armed, the crowd notices. Word moves faster than they do.
- When players look for a way into the tunnels, they find a locked maintenance hatch with recent boot marks around it and a faint chemical smell underneath.

### The Subterranean Tunnels
Old agricultural waste infrastructure repurposed into a drug lab. The piping still smells of what used to run through it. Dim work lighting. The sounds from the rave above are muffled but present.

*What's happening here:* Workers are loading the Cult shipment. They're not Bears — they're contract labour who don't know exactly what they're moving and know better than to ask.

*GM moves:*
- When players enter without authority, the workers freeze and then scatter — one of them runs for the Bear.
- When players examine the product, the batch markings don't match anything in MAPO's official inventory.
- When players threaten the workers, they give up what they know quickly — which isn't much.

### MAPO Administration Block
Clean, well-lit, the smell of recycled air and quiet bureaucracy. Separated from the mess of the rest of the station. Igor's office is at the end of a corridor lined with agricultural yield reports nobody reads.

*What's happening here:* Igor sent his assistant home early. He's waiting. He doesn't know what he's waiting for.

*GM moves:*
- When players arrive, Igor is already watching the door. He knew they'd come.
- When players ask through official channels about Alexei's death, the file has been neatly closed. Accidental overdose. Case resolved.
- When the Bear's man has already visited, Igor's office feels different — something small has been moved, and Igor sits further from the window than usual.

## NPCs

### Igor
*Concept:* MAPO administrator who works quietly against Martian interests from inside the system.

*Want:* To stay invisible. To keep his network intact. To not be the one who ends up like Alexei.

*Instinct:* Gathers information before committing to anything. Never the first one to show his cards.

*Tell:* Normally collected — slight smirk, always seems to know something you don't. Under pressure his speech speeds up, sentences pile on each other, he reaches for an exit before he's finished finding one. The smirk goes first.

*Background:* Grew up on Mars, indoctrinated like everyone else, moved to Port Vala and spent years watching the volume of food and wealth flowing into very few hands. That's what turned him. He now connects people to rebel contacts, manipulates transfer documents, and makes numbers add up in ways that aren't quite honest. Alexei was his informant. Alexei's death didn't just cost him an asset — it told him that whoever killed Alexei knows someone was listening. He hired the crew because he couldn't act through official channels without exposing everything.

### The Bear
*Concept:* The real leader of the Bears — invisible by design. Nobody in the rave knows what he looks like.

*Want:* For the operation to keep running, and for no one to ever be certain he exists.

*Instinct:* Identifies the weakest link and applies pressure there — never the strongest point.

*Tell:* Never raises his voice. Gets quieter and more precise when he's angry. He had Alexei killed without touching him. He makes every problem look like it destroyed itself. Unremarkable in person — average height, forgettable face, nothing to grab onto. That's deliberate.

### Orel *(the front man)*
*Concept:* The Bear's public face. Large, bearded, warm — everyone in the rave assumes he runs the Bears. He plays the part willingly.

*Want:* Maintain the illusion. He IS the Bear, publicly, at all times.

*Instinct:* When threatened, perform harder — more warmth, more authority, more presence. The mask becomes a weapon.

*Tell:* When the illusion starts cracking — when someone makes clear they know he's not really in charge — he escalates rather than drops it. More aggressive, more Bear-like. When the performance stops working entirely he has no backup behaviour. That's when he reaches for violence, or quietly finds a way to reach the real Bear. He knows the Bear would sacrifice him without hesitation. He tries not to think about that directly.

### Mira
*Concept:* Welfare chaplain running community support for Port Vala's foreign workforce. Has been on the station for three years. Knows everyone in the poorer parts of the station. Also the Shepherd's agent who established the cult node here — but the crew won't know that until Act 1's second beat. Full detail in `campaign/cult-of-the-shepherd.md`.

*Want:* To understand who the crew are and what they'll do before deciding how to handle them.

*Instinct:* Move toward whoever is uncertain. That's the one worth talking to.

*Tell:* She knows too much. Details of people she's met briefly, patterns she couldn't have seen without sustained attention. She asks more questions than she needs to. It reads as genuine curiosity — and some of it is.

---

### Paz
*Concept:* The Bears' drug runner for the cult. Congregation member. Addict. Coerced into killing Alexei and given his job as the price of their survival. Full encounter detail in `campaign/wolves-among-sheep.md`.

*Want:* To get through each day without stopping long enough to feel it.

*Instinct:* Comply with whoever has power. Keep moving.

*Tell:* Goes completely still when frightened — like staying small might help. Answers questions fast and completely; hesitating feels more dangerous than talking.

---

### Alexei *(dead)*
A cleaner who was siphoning from the Bears' drug shipments. The Bear found out and spiked a batch. When Alexei took his usual cut and used it, it killed him. Ruled accidental overdose. He was also Igor's informant — watching the Bears' operation and reporting what he saw.

*What he left behind:* A body with a coroner's report that doesn't quite add up, and a network of contacts who knew him and are now quietly nervous.

## Clocks

**Foreign Worker Riots**
*Fills when:* MAPO continues withholding pay and ignoring conditions; a confrontation between Martian and foreign workers turns violent.
*Consequence:* Riots, lockdown, the station in chaos. The Bears use the disruption as cover to move operations or disappear.

**Cult Being Discovered**
*Fills when:* Cult members become too visible; the drug causes a public incident; someone talks.
*Consequence:* Anti-AI crackdown. Heavy security presence across the station. The Bears sever their Cult connection before the crew can trace it — and the Shepherd is warned.
