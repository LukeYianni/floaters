# The Rave

A basement venue beneath the Burnt Market. The Bears run it as a front — a genuine club that also happens to cover everything happening underneath it. Exhaust fumes and spilled alcohol. Low ceilings. The bass is loud enough that the walls shake and conversation requires leaning in close. It feels less like a club and more like a pub that gave up on being a pub.

Most people here are just having a night. That's the point.

---

## Layout

Five areas: the main floor, the bar, Orel's booth, the guarded door, and below it — the operational level with four rooms.

---

## The Main Floor

Packed. Dim. Strobing light that makes it hard to read faces. The crowd is mostly foreign workforce — temporary workers with nowhere better to be and no reason to ask questions.

Five or six Bears are embedded here as staff: on the doors, moving through the crowd, behind the bar. They look like bouncers and bartenders because they are — the cover is real.

*What happens here:*
- When players ask around, they get half-truths. People know the Bears run this place. Nobody knows what's underneath it.
- When players move toward the guarded door, a Bear clocks it within thirty seconds and positions between them and it.
- When violence breaks out on the floor, the crowd panics — cover and chaos in equal measure. The Bears use both.
- When players look for Orel, they find him immediately. He's not hiding.

---

## The Bar

Sticky floor. Overworked bartender who is definitely a Bear and definitely not friendly. The bar itself is long enough that it creates a natural barrier between the crowd and the staff area behind it.

*What's interesting:* Behind the bar, under the counter, is a radio handset. It's how the bar staff signal the back. If players get hold of it, they can hear everything the Bears say to each other during the night — and transmit on the same frequency.

*GM moves:*
- When players try to get behind the bar, the bartender doesn't move. He just watches them.
- When players take the radio, a Bear notices within minutes that check-ins have stopped.

---

## Orel's Booth

A raised corner at the back of the main floor. Orel can see the whole room from here. More importantly, the whole room can see him — that's the point. A bottle on the table, always company, always performing. Two Bears nearby who look like friends but aren't.

*What's interesting:* Orel is the first point of contact. He'll talk to the crew — he enjoys it. He'll buy them a drink. The conversation is useful: he gives away more than he intends when he's performing well, and the crew can learn that he defers to something he doesn't name when the questions get specific.

*GM moves:*
- When players push on who's really in charge, Orel escalates the performance — louder, warmer, more present. He doesn't answer.
- When players threaten him here, in public, he smiles. He has the room.
- When players say something that makes it clear they know he's not in charge, watch his hands. They go still.

---

## The Guarded Door

A heavy door at the back of the venue. Two Bears on it, big enough that nobody mistakes them for regular staff. They'll say it's storage. They won't move.

The door opens into a short corridor — bare metal walls, a single overhead light, crates stacked against one side. Functional, unremarkable. At the far end, a second door and a stairwell going down. The corridor is narrow enough that anyone who gets through the first door without permission is in a kill box before they reach the second.

*GM moves:*
- When players try to talk their way through, the guards listen politely and don't move.
- When players get through by force or distraction, they have about ninety seconds before someone radios it in.
- When players are in the corridor and the alarm goes up, both doors lock.

---

## Below: The Operational Level

Four rooms. This is where the Bears actually live and work.

---

### The Common Room

Tables, mismatched chairs, food wrappers, a screen showing station news on mute. Smells like takeout and unwashed clothing. Lived-in to the point of being grimy. This is where Bears off-shift eat, sleep, argue, and decompress.

*What's interesting:* A shift roster is pinned to the wall — names, times, positions. It tells the crew exactly how many Bears are here tonight and where each one is supposed to be. Cross-referenced with who they've already seen, it also tells them if anyone's unaccounted for.

There's also a Bear in here right now, off-shift, eating alone. He's young. He doesn't look like he wants to be here. He will give up everything he knows if the crew frightens him enough — which isn't much.

*GM moves:*
- When players read the roster, they can identify a gap — someone who should be somewhere and isn't.
- When players pressure the off-shift Bear, he breaks quickly. What he knows: the tunnel access is in the secure store, the real boss isn't Orel, and there's a shipment going out tonight.
- When players let him go, he goes straight to Orel.

---

### The Secure Store

Locked. Shelving units with product, a cash box, a weapons rack with two shotguns and ammunition. The tunnel access hatch is in the floor at the back — heavy, padlocked, but the padlock key is hanging on a hook by the door as if that's completely fine.

*What's interesting:* Three things of value here beyond the weapons:

**The Cult shipment** — packaged, labeled, ready to move. The label has a docking code for a ship that doesn't appear in any Port Vala registry. This is the thread to Act 2. If the crew takes it or photographs it, they have a lead to the hidden station.

**A separate batch, differently labeled** — smaller, marked with a single red line. This is the spiked batch. The same formulation that killed Alexei. It's evidence of murder, sitting in a locked room.

**A physical ledger** — financial records in simple code. If decoded (not difficult with time), it shows payments to two MAPO administrators above Igor. The Bears have reach into the administration that Igor doesn't know about.

*GM moves:*
- When players take the Cult shipment, the courier comes looking for it within the hour.
- When players take the ledger, the Bear notices it's gone before they're off the station.
- When players open the tunnel hatch, the smell of chemical processing comes up from below.

---

### The Bear's Office

Small. Tidy — the only tidy room down here. A desk, a terminal, a chair. Nothing personal. No photographs, no decoration, nothing that says anything about who uses this room. That absence is itself information.

*What's interesting:* The terminal is locked but not encrypted. If players get in, they find two things:

**Message thread with the Cult** — sparse, coded, but the pattern is clear. Regular check-ins. The most recent message from the Cult's end is different in tone from the others — shorter, more urgent. Something has changed on their end.

**A second exit** — a panel behind the desk that opens into a maintenance shaft. The Bear always has an exit. It connects to the station's general maintenance network, which eventually reaches the docking level.

*GM moves:*
- When players read the Cult messages, they don't get a location — but they get enough to know the Shepherd is aware something is wrong on Port Vala.
- When players find the second exit, they realise the Bear may have already used it.
- When players search the room thoroughly, they find nothing personal. That's the most unsettling thing in it.

---

### The Communications Setup

A bank of screens showing feeds from cameras placed around the station: the docking bay, the entrance to MAPO administration, several corridors in the Burnt Market, the front door of the rave. The Bears have been watching the station — and the crew — since they arrived.

*What's interesting:* One feed shows Igor's building. Players can see, right now, whether Igor is still in his office or whether the lights are out.

A second feed — not a camera, but a live audio channel — carries intermittent chatter from somewhere else on the station. It takes a moment to place: it's the Workers' Union meeting hall. The Bears have a microphone in there. They've been listening to the Union organise.

*GM moves:*
- When players review the footage, they can see themselves on camera from earlier in the night — the Bears knew exactly where they were and when.
- When players use the comms setup to monitor the rave above, they hear Orel asking where the crew went.
- When players pull the Union audio, they hear something that suggests the Workers' Union knows more about the Bears' operation than they should.
