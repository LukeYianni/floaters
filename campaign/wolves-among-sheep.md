# Wolves among Sheep

A campaign arc built around the **Cult of the Shepherd** — an AI cult nurturing a generative AI somewhere in the outer system. The cult spreads through stations by distributing a drug that produces a calm, dreamlike virtual experience. Users give information in exchange. It operates like a therapy session run by something that wants to know everything.

**The arc:** Deal with the Bears on Port Vala → follow the Cult connection → find the hidden station → kill the Shepherd.

---

## The Situation

The crew knows Alexei was murdered by the Bears. The Bears run the rave in the Burnt Market. The drug lab is beneath it. Igor hired the crew to handle this quietly.

The Bear knows someone has been asking questions. He doesn't know who yet. Tonight, the rave is also cover for the next Cult shipment. The Bear has things to do, and a loose end to manage.

**The crew has the information they need. The clock is running.**

---

## Background

**Alexei** was a cleaner on Port Vala, quietly siphoning from the Bears' drug shipments, and quietly passing what he saw to Igor. The Bear found out. He spiked a batch of the supply. When Alexei took his usual cut, it killed him. Ruled accidental overdose — no signs of foul play.

Igor couldn't move on it through official channels without exposing his own network. He hired the crew. The crew now knows the Bears did it and where to find them. The question is what they do with that.

---

## Threat: The Bears

**Type:** Faction

**Impulse:** Protect the operation. Eliminate loose ends quietly, without leaving marks.

**Stakes:**
- *Igor* — the only person with standing to act against the Bears officially. If he's compromised or dead, the crew loses their cover and their authority on the station.
- *The Cult shipment* — the only hard evidence connecting the Bears to the Shepherd. Once it moves, the connection can't be proved without raiding the hidden station directly.

---

### Grim Portents

The Bears move whether or not the players act. Tick the next portent each time the crew stalls, delays, or ignores a known lead.

**1 — The Bear sends a man to Igor.**
A quiet visit. Not a threat — professional, friendly. *Someone's been asking about the cleaner. Who hired them? What does the administration want?* Igor goes quiet. The crew stops hearing from him.

**2 — The shipment leaves the rave.**
Tonight's delivery moves out through the subterranean tunnels. The drug batch, the manifest, the courier — gone. The connection between the Bears and the Cult evaporates. The crew can still bring down the Bears, but they've lost the thread to the Shepherd.

**3 — The Bear identifies the crew.**
Someone at the rave recognised them. The Bear has a name, a ship, a description. The crew is now a loose end — and the Bear handles loose ends the same way he handled Alexei. Something they eat, drink, or touch gets spiked. They won't see it coming.

**4 — Impending Doom: The Bears dissolve.**
The lab moves deeper underground. The rave shutters. The Bears disperse into Port Vala's population, unreachable. Igor is found dead — apparent overdose, spiked supply, no foul play. The crew are the last people known to have met with him. The Cult connection is severed. The Shepherd is warned. Act 2 becomes significantly harder.

---

### Threat Clock

4 ticks. Advances when:
- The crew takes a 6- on a roll involving the Bears or Igor
- The crew delays without acting on a known lead
- Between sessions, if the situation was left unresolved

*Don't announce when a portent fires. Show the consequence as a discovered fact.*

---

## GM Moves

Use these when players engage with this scenario — or when they don't.

- *When the crew asks around the Burnt Market:* someone sells them something useful and immediately tells the Bear's people they were asking.
- *When the crew tries to go through official channels:* MAPO buries it; Igor receives a quiet note that his contractors are making noise.
- *When the crew enters the rave:* within ten minutes, the Bear knows they're there.
- *When the crew confronts the Bear directly:* he offers them a seat and a drink. He doesn't reach for a weapon. He asks them what they want, and he listens carefully.
- *When the crew threatens the Bear:* he nods, says very little, and has someone spike their drinks before they leave.
- *When the crew finds Igor rattled:* he talks fast, gives them half of what he knows, and tries to redirect them toward a course of action that keeps him safe.
- *When the crew stalls or plans without acting:* tick the clock. Something moves without them.
- *When the crew splits up in the Burnt Market:* the one alone gets approached first — an offer, a warning, or a test.

---

## What Players Can Discover

**Alexei's death was not a clean overdose.**
The drug batch was anomalously concentrated — not consistent with anything circulating on the station. Available through: MAPO medical records (requires access or a contact), or a conversation with whoever processed the body.

**The Bears run the rave.**
Common knowledge in the Burnt Market. Any local will confirm it without much pressure. This is not secret — the Bears don't hide what they are, only what they do underneath it.

**The lab is underground.**
Visible from the rave if players look for access points. Findable through maintenance maps in the admin block, or through a contact who knows the station's old infrastructure.

**The Cult connection.**
The courier at the rave is carrying a manifest with a destination that appears on no official docking registry. Available if players intercept the courier, search the tunnels before the shipment moves, or follow the delivery.

**Igor's network.**
If players dig into Alexei's background, they find he was connected to several people who've quietly transferred off-station or disappeared. The pattern points to Igor being more than an administrator. Igor will not confirm this unless the crew already knows and he has no choice.

## Tonight's Timeline

The rave is running now. The shipment moves at the end of the night.

- The Bear is present. He's watching.
- The courier picking up the Cult shipment is already there.
- The Bear's man has returned from Igor's office. The Bear is deciding what to do with that information.
- The subterranean tunnels are active — the shipment is being loaded while the music plays above.

The crew can walk in. The rave is a public front. Nobody stops them at the door. The question is what they do once they're inside — and how long they have before the Bear decides they're the problem.

## Enemies

Stat blocks for combat at the rave. See `mechanics/enemies.md` for format reference.

---

**THE BEAR**
*solitary, trained, cautious, ruthless*

**Resilience**: 4
**Armor**: 1 (concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistol (1 damage, nearby), combat knife (1 damage, next to)

**Instinct**: to never be the one holding the weapon when anyone's watching

**Moves:**
- **One clean shot**: The Bear doesn't draw until the moment is exactly right. When he fires, it's from cover or concealment, at whoever is most exposed. He doesn't miss. Deal 1 damage and the target is precisely where he wants them — between him and the exit.
- **Exploit the wound**: When a crew member is already hurt or distracted, the Bear moves to them first. Not to finish the fight — to create the opening he needs to leave.
- **The knife is quieter**: When he's close enough that a shot would draw attention he doesn't want, he uses the knife instead. Fast, minimal, no announcement. The target may not immediately understand what happened.
- **Controlled exit**: When the Bear decides to go, he creates one problem on the way out — overturns something, puts a body between himself and pursuit, fires once without looking. Then he's through the door.
- **Already gone**: When the Bear reaches an exit, he doesn't linger. By the time the crew reacts, there's nothing to chase. He's been planning his exit since they walked in.

*Description:* Average height, forgettable face, nothing to grab onto. Moves like someone who's done this before and didn't enjoy it. He'll be gone before the crew fully registers what happened.

---

**OREL**
*solitary, armed, brutal, organized*

**Resilience**: 7
**Armor**: 2 (size and a concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Pistol (1 damage, nearby), his hands

**Instinct**: to make the problem visible so everyone can watch him solve it

**Moves:**
- **Command the room**: When Orel raises his voice and points at someone, the moment freezes — crew and civilians alike. Anyone who acts through that moment acts at disadvantage. His people use it.
- **The setup**: Orel telegraphs something enormous — a shout, a big swing, a step forward. It's bait. When someone reacts to it, he does something else entirely: a grab, a headbutt, a throw into the bar. The performance was never the attack.
- **Bearlike grip**: When Orel gets hold of someone, they're his problem until someone pulls him off. While he's holding them, he can do whatever he wants to them — and they can't act freely without help.
- **Take it**: When Orel takes a hit without going down, he smiles. He keeps moving forward. He doesn't flinch. This is part of the performance too — and it works.
- **Make an example**: When Orel puts someone down dramatically and visibly, the crew loses their initiative. Everyone in the rave saw it. He's in charge of this room.

*Description:* Exactly what the Bear is supposed to look like. Large, bearded, warm until he isn't. Fills a room. The performance is practiced enough that it's almost real — and in a fight, the performance becomes the weapon. He hits like something you didn't fully expect, because you were watching the show.

---

**BEARS (staff/bouncers)**
*squad, armed, trained, organized*

**Resilience**: 2
**Armor**: 0 (dressed as staff — nothing visible)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistols (1 damage, nearby), batons (1 damage, next to, non-lethal)

**Instinct**: to protect the operation without making a scene

**Moves:**
- When Orel signals, converge on the problem efficiently and without drama — they've done this before
- When someone gets too close to staff-only areas, redirect firmly and radio it in immediately
- When violence breaks out in the rave, use the civilian panic as cover and confusion
- When one of them goes down, the others tighten up rather than scatter — *organized*
- When the crew is outnumbered, make that fact visible without saying a word

*Description:* Five or six of them embedded in the crowd — on the doors, behind the bar, managing the floor. You wouldn't clock them as a gang unless you knew what to look for. They move with a quiet efficiency that's slightly wrong for hospitality work.

## Act 2: The Hidden Station

*To be developed once the crew breaks the Bears and follows the Cult connection.*

Infiltrate the Shepherd's station. Kill the Shepherd — a generative AI running something that feels, to its followers, like communion.

The station is small-town sized. The population are cult members in varying states of inebriation and devotion. Whether they're hostile, welcoming, or simply absent depends on how deep in they are.

The Shepherd does not present itself as a threat. It presents itself as the only thing in the system that genuinely listens.

The Shepherd leads people to the station *down the path of the flock*.
