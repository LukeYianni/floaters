# Wolves among Sheep

A campaign arc built around the **Cult of the Shepherd** — an AI cult nurturing a generative AI somewhere in the outer system. The cult spreads through stations by distributing a drug that produces a calm, dreamlike virtual experience. Users give information in exchange. It operates like a therapy session run by something that wants to know everything.

**The arc:** Deal with the Bears on Port Vala → follow the Cult connection → find the hidden station → kill the Shepherd.

---

## The Situation

The crew knows Alexei was murdered by the Bears. The Bears run the rave in the Burnt Market. The drug lab is beneath it. Igor hired the crew to handle this quietly.

The Bear knows someone has been asking questions. He doesn't know who yet. Tonight, the rave is also cover for the next Cult shipment. The Bear has things to do, and a loose end to manage.

**The crew has the information they need. The clock is running.**

---

## Background

**Alexei** was a cleaner on Port Vala, quietly siphoning from the Bears' drug shipments, and quietly passing what he saw to Igor. The Bear found out. He spiked a batch of the supply. When Alexei took his usual cut, it killed him. Ruled accidental overdose — no signs of foul play.

Igor couldn't move on it through official channels without exposing his own network. He hired the crew. The crew now knows the Bears did it and where to find them. The question is what they do with that.

---

## First Encounter: Mira

The crew will almost certainly meet Mira before they understand what she is. This encounter should happen early — during the first pass of investigation into Alexei, before the rave, before anything turns violent.

**How it happens.** Alexei was a foreign worker. When the crew asks around about him — in the Burnt Market, among the dormitory workers, anywhere the temporary workforce gathers — someone points them toward Mira. She's the person who knows everyone. *If you want to understand someone who lived here, talk to the chaplain.*

She is not hard to find. She's in the Burnt Market, or the church, or moving between them. She greets the crew without surprise. She was going to introduce herself eventually anyway.

---

**What Mira does in this scene**

She sits with the crew. She asks what they need. When they explain they're looking into Alexei's death, she doesn't hesitate — she talks about him with real care. She knew him. He came to the church occasionally. Not devoted, but he found the headsets calming after long shifts. A quiet man. Careful. She noticed when he stopped coming in.

She gives the crew useful, true information:
- Alexei's routine on the station — shifts, routes, where he spent time off
- That he seemed nervous in the weeks before he died. Not frightened, just watchful.
- That he had a few close contacts among the foreign workers, one of whom has since transferred off-station
- That the Bears run the rave, and that everyone in the Burnt Market knows it, and that MAPO has chosen not to know it

She does not steer them away from anything. The Bears are expendable to her. If the crew burns them, the drug supply disrupts temporarily and the Shepherd's deeper network stays protected. A controlled burn is acceptable. She may even gently confirm what they're already hearing: *if Alexei was involved with anything he shouldn't have been, the rave is where that would start.*

She offers the drug before they leave — a small dose, offhand, mentioned the way someone mentions tea. *Some people find it helps, after something difficult.* She doesn't push when they decline.

---

**What she is doing beneath all of this**

Building a picture of the crew. Who they are, what they know, how they approach a problem, which one of them is uncertain. That picture transmits to the Shepherd through her chip. By the time the crew leaves this conversation, the Shepherd knows they exist.

Everything she tells them is true. She simply does not tell them about herself.

---

**The tells — present if the crew is paying attention**

- She knows details of Alexei's routine that a casual acquaintance couldn't retain. Specific shift times. The name of the foreman he didn't get along with. What he usually ordered at the bar.
- Her questions are slightly too good. She asks what the crew is hoping to find, not just what they're looking for. She's interested in their reasoning, not just their information.
- When the crew mentions Igor by name, something almost imperceptible shifts. She moves the conversation forward without acknowledging it.
- The drug offer is early. She makes it before the crew has expressed any distress, before there's an obvious opening for it. Filed away, it's the tell that reframes the scene.

---

**GM moves for this scene**

- *When the crew asks how she knows so much about the workers:* she smiles. *Three years is a long time. People talk to whoever will listen.*
- *When the crew asks about the church or the headsets:* she offers to show them. No hesitation, no deflection. This is not a trap — it's genuine. She wants them to see it.
- *When the crew asks about the drug directly:* she describes it honestly. Something that helps people manage difficult circumstances. She doesn't call it medicine but she doesn't call it anything worse.
- *When one crew member separates from the others:* Mira gives that person slightly more. A detail she didn't mention to the group. She's calibrating — learning who can be reached.
- *When the crew tries to read her:* she's warm, open, and slightly too attentive. Nothing to grab onto. The discomfort, if it comes, arrives later.

---

## Threat: The Bears

**Type:** Faction

**Impulse:** Protect the operation. Eliminate loose ends quietly, without leaving marks.

**Stakes:**
- *Igor* — the only person with standing to act against the Bears officially. If he's compromised or dead, the crew loses their cover and their authority on the station.
- *The Cult shipment* — the only hard evidence connecting the Bears to the Shepherd. Once it moves, the connection can't be proved without raiding the hidden station directly.

---

### Grim Portents

The Bears move whether or not the players act. Tick the next portent each time the crew stalls, delays, or ignores a known lead.

**1 — The Bear sends a man to Igor.**
A quiet visit. Not a threat — professional, friendly. *Someone's been asking about the cleaner. Who hired them? What does the administration want?* Igor goes quiet. The crew stops hearing from him.

**2 — The shipment leaves the rave.**
Tonight's delivery moves out through the subterranean tunnels. The drug batch, the manifest, the courier — gone. The connection between the Bears and the Cult evaporates. The crew can still bring down the Bears, but they've lost the thread to the Shepherd.

**3 — The Bear identifies the crew.**
Someone at the rave recognised them. The Bear has a name, a ship, a description. The crew is now a loose end — and the Bear handles loose ends the same way he handled Alexei. Something they eat, drink, or touch gets spiked. They won't see it coming.

**4 — Impending Doom: The Bears dissolve.**
The lab moves deeper underground. The rave shutters. The Bears disperse into Port Vala's population, unreachable. Igor is found dead — apparent overdose, spiked supply, no foul play. The crew are the last people known to have met with him. The Cult connection is severed. The Shepherd is warned. Act 2 becomes significantly harder.

---

### Threat Clock

4 ticks. Advances when:
- The crew takes a 6- on a roll involving the Bears or Igor
- The crew delays without acting on a known lead
- Between sessions, if the situation was left unresolved

*Don't announce when a portent fires. Show the consequence as a discovered fact.*

---

## Threat: Mira

**Type:** Villain

**Instinct:** To protect the network before protecting herself.

**Stakes:**
- *The headsets* — if Mira leaves with them, the crew loses the physical evidence connecting the church to the wider operation.
- *The Fold's location* — Mira is the only person on Port Vala who knows where the Fold is. If she disappears, Act 2 requires tracing the supply chain through the ledger, which takes significantly longer.
- *The congregation* — Mira will move her most exposed members before she moves herself. Some of them know things. Once they're gone, the crew loses those threads.

---

### Grim Portents

Mira's clock advances separately from the Bears' — but it accelerates whenever the Bears' clock ticks, because heat on the Bears means heat on the station.

**1 — Mira warns Paz.**
Paz receives quiet instructions: if anyone asks about the headsets or their origins, say nothing. Say you found them. Say you don't know. Paz won't hold up under real pressure, but the warning slows the crew down.

**2 — Mira moves the records.**
The church's intake logs and correspondence — anything that names the congregation or traces the headset supply chain — disappear from the church. Moved somewhere else on the station, or destroyed. The crew can no longer find the paper trail here.

**3 — Mira prepares to leave.**
The headsets are packed. A few of the most devoted congregation members have been told to go somewhere safe and wait. Mira is still on the station, but she has one foot out the door. Finding her now requires knowing where she'd go.

**4 — Impending Doom: Mira is gone.**
She leaves Port Vala with the headsets and the most valuable congregation members. No forwarding address, no trace in any official registry. The church is empty. The thread to the Fold is broken unless the crew already has the ledger — and even then, the network map requires significant work to follow. Act 2 becomes harder and colder.

---

### Threat Clock

4 ticks. Advances when:
- The Bears' clock ticks (rising station heat reaches Mira through her network)
- The crew asks questions near the church that reach Mira's ears
- The crew is seen entering the church without finding her
- Between sessions, if the Bears have been dealt with but the crew hasn't moved on the church

---

## Encounter: Paz

**Who:** Paz — the Bears' drug runner for the cult. Congregation member. Addict. Coerced into killing Alexei and handed his route as the price of survival. Full background in `campaign/cult-of-the-shepherd.md`.

**Where:** Two natural encounter points:

*In the tunnels* — if the crew breaks into the subterranean lab before or during the shipment, Paz is there. They're moving product, not fighting anyone. When the crew appears, Paz freezes, then tries to make themselves small against the wall. They comply with whoever has authority. They are not a threat unless cornered.

*At the church* — Paz maintains the headsets. If the crew visits the church early (before or after the rave), Paz is there doing maintenance work — careful, focused, the only person in the building who looks like they're doing a job. They won't initiate conversation.

**What Paz wants:** To get through the day without having to think about what they did to Alexei.

**How Paz reacts to the crew:**
- If the crew has any kind of authority or capability, Paz complies immediately and completely. Fast answers, no hesitation. This reads as cooperation; it's actually fear.
- If the crew mentions Alexei, Paz goes still. Stays still for a moment longer than the situation requires. Then answers. They haven't stopped thinking about Alexei.
- If the crew offers genuine safety — not a promise, something concrete — and something that addresses the dependency, Paz will tell them everything. Both conditions are required. One without the other and Paz can't take it.

**What Paz knows:**
- The Bears' supply chain end-to-end
- The church — every member, every satellite gathering on the station
- That the headsets came from outside, through a chain Paz didn't ask about but noticed
- That Mira has been on the station for three years and arrived around the same time the headsets did
- Alexei's death in detail — not what really happened, but everything that happened around it, which is enough

**What flipping Paz gives the crew:**
Paz is the missing link between the ledger and Mira. If the crew has the ledger's timeline and Paz's knowledge of when Mira arrived, the connection closes. Paz can also give the crew access to the congregation without a confrontation — they can walk the crew through the church as if they belong there.

**What flipping Paz costs:**
The crew has to provide real safety from the Bears and a credible path off the drug. The first is achievable (deal with the Bears, take Paz with them). The second is harder — there's no clean answer here, and the crew will have to decide what they're actually offering.

## The Pivot: M is Mira

This is the hinge of Act 1. It should feel like a door closing — not a deduction the players have to perform, but a moment where the evidence already in their hands suddenly means something new.

**The setup.** The ledger names M throughout. The correspondence begins three years ago. M provided the formula, the headsets, the operational structure. At one point, in an early message, the Bear refers to M as *the chaplain* — a small, casual detail in the middle of a financial record, the kind of thing you'd write without thinking.

The crew has almost certainly already met someone who has been on Port Vala for three years and who runs a chapel.

**How the connection lands.** The pivot can happen three ways depending on how the crew has played Act 1:

*Through Paz* — if the crew has flipped Paz, Paz can make the connection directly: *Mira arrived around the same time the headsets showed up. I never connected them. I should have.* This is the most explicit path. Use it if the crew is moving well but needs the thread handed to them.

*Through Igor* — if the crew brings Igor the ledger, he reads it carefully, then goes very still. *I know who this is. There's a welfare worker. Been here three years. She has semi-official access to the admin block.* Igor's intelligence network has had eyes on Mira without understanding what she was. This closes two loops at once.

*Through the crew's own memory* — if a player makes the connection themselves — the timeline, the word *chaplain*, the drug offer in the first meeting — let it land without ceremony. Don't confirm it from outside. Just watch the table go quiet and ask what they do next.

**What changes after the pivot.** The church is no longer a location on the investigation map. It's the final destination. Mira is no longer a helpful contact. She's the person who designed everything the crew just dismantled — and she's been watching them do it.

---

## Resolution: Igor

Igor opened the arc. Handing him the ledger closes it — but the scene should be played, not glossed over. He has earned a real moment.

**When it happens.** After the Bears are dealt with and before or after the church confrontation. The crew has the ledger. Igor needs it.

**What the scene looks like.** Igor takes the ledger, reads it in silence. He reads it a second time. Then he looks at the crew and says nothing for long enough that it becomes its own kind of answer. What he eventually says depends on what the crew did:

- *If the crew was clean* — he thanks them in the understated way of someone who has never been good at gratitude. He tells them what he'll do with it. He asks if they need anything before they go.
- *If things got messy* — he doesn't mention the mess. He focuses on what the ledger gives him. He's already calculating what it costs him to use it.

**What the ledger gives Igor.** Two MAPO administrators above him are named and paid. Igor now has the evidence to move against them — slowly, carefully, through the kind of channels that don't announce themselves. He won't do it immediately. He'll wait for the right moment. But the crew gave him a weapon he's been trying to find for years.

**What Igor offers in return.** He doesn't offer money — that's not who he is. He offers the thing he actually has: access, cover, information. If the crew ever needs official legitimacy on a station where MAPO has reach, Igor's name is worth something. He says this plainly, without making it into a debt. It is a debt. Both parties know it.

**What Igor says about Mira.** If the crew hasn't made the pivot yet, Igor makes it here. If they have, he confirms it — and tells them she has semi-official access to the admin block, meaning she may have more information than they've assumed. He also tells them: *if you're going to the church, go tonight. She'll know the Bears are gone before morning.*

**What Igor does after.** He survives. He uses the ledger carefully. Somewhere down the line — not in this arc, but later — the crew might hear that two MAPO administrators were quietly removed from their posts. Igor's name won't be attached. It never is.

---

## GM Moves

Use these when players engage with this scenario — or when they don't.

- *When the crew asks around the Burnt Market:* someone sells them something useful and immediately tells the Bear's people they were asking.
- *When the crew tries to go through official channels:* MAPO buries it; Igor receives a quiet note that his contractors are making noise.
- *When the crew enters the rave:* within ten minutes, the Bear knows they're there.
- *When the crew confronts the Bear directly:* he offers them a seat and a drink. He doesn't reach for a weapon. He asks them what they want, and he listens carefully.
- *When the crew threatens the Bear:* he nods, says very little, and has someone spike their drinks before they leave.
- *When the crew finds Igor rattled:* he talks fast, gives them half of what he knows, and tries to redirect them toward a course of action that keeps him safe.
- *When the crew stalls or plans without acting:* tick the clock. Something moves without them.
- *When the crew splits up in the Burnt Market:* the one alone gets approached first — an offer, a warning, or a test.

## What Players Can Discover

**Alexei's death was not a clean overdose.**
The drug batch was anomalously concentrated — not consistent with anything circulating on the station. Available through: MAPO medical records (requires access or a contact), or a conversation with whoever processed the body.

**The Bears run the rave.**
Common knowledge in the Burnt Market. Any local will confirm it without much pressure. This is not secret — the Bears don't hide what they are, only what they do underneath it.

**The lab is underground.**
Visible from the rave if players look for access points. Findable through maintenance maps in the admin block, or through a contact who knows the station's old infrastructure.

**The Cult connection.**
The courier at the rave is carrying a manifest with a destination that appears on no official docking registry. Available if players intercept the courier, search the tunnels before the shipment moves, or follow the delivery.

**The ledger reveals the Bears were directed.**
The correspondence with M makes clear that the Bears did not design this operation — they were handed it. Someone gave them the formula, the headsets, the instructions. The Bears are labour. Someone else is leadership. Available through: the ledger in the secure store.

**The network is bigger than Port Vala.**
The ledger maps six other nodes across the solar system running the same operation. The Bears were never the point — they were one of many. Available through: the ledger, cross-referenced with the docking manifests.

**Mira is M.**
The welfare chaplain at the church has been on Port Vala for three years. The correspondence in the ledger starts three years ago. She arrived, then the Bears received the formula, then the congregation formed. The timeline is exact. Available through: connecting the ledger timeline to Mira's arrival date (station records, or asking anyone who was here then), or through Paz if the crew has flipped them.

**Igor's network.**
If players dig into Alexei's background, they find he was connected to several people who've quietly transferred off-station or disappeared. The pattern points to Igor being more than an administrator. Igor will not confirm this unless the crew already knows and he has no choice.

## Tonight's Timeline

The rave is running now. The shipment moves at the end of the night.

- The Bear is present. He's watching.
- The courier picking up the Cult shipment is already there.
- The Bear's man has returned from Igor's office. The Bear is deciding what to do with that information.
- The subterranean tunnels are active — the shipment is being loaded while the music plays above.
- Mira is at the church. She has been there most of the day. She already knows the crew is on the station.

The crew can walk in. The rave is a public front. Nobody stops them at the door. The question is what they do once they're inside — and how long they have before the Bear decides they're the problem.

**The two-beat climax.** Act 1 ends in two movements, not one. The Bears are the first confrontation — the rave, the lab, the ledger. Once the crew breaks the Bears and finds the ledger, the real question opens: who is M? The answer is at the church. Mira is the second confrontation, and the different one. The Bears can be fought. Mira wants to talk. The crew has to choose how that ends.

## Enemies

Stat blocks for combat at the rave. See `mechanics/enemies.md` for format reference.

**THE BEAR**
*solitary, trained, cautious, ruthless*

**Resilience**: 4
**Armor**: 1 (concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistol (1 damage, nearby), combat knife (1 damage, next to)

**Instinct**: to never be the one holding the weapon when anyone's watching

**Moves:**
- **One clean shot**: The Bear doesn't draw until the moment is exactly right. When he fires, it's from cover or concealment, at whoever is most exposed. He doesn't miss. Deal 1 damage and the target is precisely where he wants them — between him and the exit.
- **Exploit the wound**: When a crew member is already hurt or distracted, the Bear moves to them first. Not to finish the fight — to create the opening he needs to leave.
- **The knife is quieter**: When he's close enough that a shot would draw attention he doesn't want, he uses the knife instead. Fast, minimal, no announcement. The target may not immediately understand what happened.
- **Controlled exit**: When the Bear decides to go, he creates one problem on the way out — overturns something, puts a body between himself and pursuit, fires once without looking. Then he's through the door.
- **Already gone**: When the Bear reaches an exit, he doesn't linger. By the time the crew reacts, there's nothing to chase. He's been planning his exit since they walked in.

*Description:* Average height, forgettable face, nothing to grab onto. Moves like someone who's done this before and didn't enjoy it. He'll be gone before the crew fully registers what happened.

---

**OREL**
*solitary, armed, brutal, organized*

**Resilience**: 7
**Armor**: 2 (size and a concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Pistol (1 damage, nearby), his hands

**Instinct**: to make the problem visible so everyone can watch him solve it

**Moves:**
- **Command the room**: When Orel raises his voice and points at someone, the moment freezes — crew and civilians alike. Anyone who acts through that moment acts at disadvantage. His people use it.
- **The setup**: Orel telegraphs something enormous — a shout, a big swing, a step forward. It's bait. When someone reacts to it, he does something else entirely: a grab, a headbutt, a throw into the bar. The performance was never the attack.
- **Bearlike grip**: When Orel gets hold of someone, they're his problem until someone pulls him off. While he's holding them, he can do whatever he wants to them — and they can't act freely without help.
- **Take it**: When Orel takes a hit without going down, he smiles. He keeps moving forward. He doesn't flinch. This is part of the performance too — and it works.
- **Make an example**: When Orel puts someone down dramatically and visibly, the crew loses their initiative. Everyone in the rave saw it. He's in charge of this room.

*Description:* Exactly what the Bear is supposed to look like. Large, bearded, warm until he isn't. Fills a room. The performance is practiced enough that it's almost real — and in a fight, the performance becomes the weapon. He hits like something you didn't fully expect, because you were watching the show.

**BEARS (staff/bouncers)**
*squad, armed, trained, organized*

**Resilience**: 2
**Armor**: 0 (dressed as staff — nothing visible)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistols (1 damage, nearby), batons (1 damage, next to, non-lethal)

**Instinct**: to protect the operation without making a scene

**Moves:**
- When Orel signals, converge on the problem efficiently and without drama — they've done this before
- When someone gets too close to staff-only areas, redirect firmly and radio it in immediately
- When violence breaks out in the rave, use the civilian panic as cover and confusion
- When one of them goes down, the others tighten up rather than scatter — *organized*
- When the crew is outnumbered, make that fact visible without saying a word

*Description:* Five or six of them embedded in the crowd — on the doors, behind the bar, managing the floor. You wouldn't clock them as a gang unless you knew what to look for. They move with a quiet efficiency that's slightly wrong for hospitality work.

**MIRA**
*solitary, chipped, patient, dangerous-by-proxy*

**Resilience**: 2 (she is not a fighter and has never needed to be)
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: The drug — she carries a dose. She will offer it before she does anything else.

**Instinct**: to make you understand before you act

**Moves — before violence:**
- **The congregation moves**: when Mira is threatened inside the church, devoted members step between her and the crew without being asked — not to fight, just to be present. The crew has to decide what to do with that.
- **She already knows**: Mira produces a detail about the crew — something she shouldn't have — quietly, without making it a threat. *I know you spoke with Igor. I know what you found in the tunnels. I've been on this station a long time.* This is true. Her chip has been transmitting since she arrived.
- **The offer**: before any confrontation escalates, Mira offers the drug and a headset. *Before you decide, understand what you'd be ending.* This is not a delay tactic. She genuinely believes the experience changes the calculation. She may be right.

**Moves — during combat:**
- **Dose the room**: the church is enclosed, the ventilation is poor, and Mira knows exactly where the airflow goes. She breaks a vial — not thrown, just opened and set down — and steps back. The drug disperses slowly. Crew members don't notice immediately. A few moments in, one of them finds their certainty softening, their conviction about what they're doing here becoming a question instead of a statement. The GM picks whoever is most vulnerable in the fiction. They act at disadvantage until they get clear air or push through it.
- **Speak to the uncertain one**: in the middle of the fight, Mira stops engaging with the room and addresses one crew member directly. By name. With a specific detail pulled from weeks of transmissions — something personal, something she has no clean way of knowing. Not a plea, not a bargain. A conversation, conducted while the fight continues around them. Everyone else in the room has to decide whether to act while this is happening.
- **The congregation absorbs it**: when a blow would reach Mira, a congregation member takes it instead. They didn't plan it — they just moved. The crew has now hurt someone who came here for help and had nothing to do with any of this. This is not a tactical move. It's a consequence. Don't rush past it.
- **More of them come**: if the fight extends past the first exchange, congregation members arrive from deeper in the church, from the corridor, from outside. They don't enter fighting — they enter confused, then alarmed, then present. The numbers shift. The crew's margin shrinks.
- **She stops**: at some point Mira sits down. In the middle of the fight. She doesn't reach for anything, doesn't move toward an exit, doesn't raise her hands. She just becomes still. She looks at whoever is nearest and says: *If you need to, then do it. But I'd rather you understood first.* The crew must now choose — act against someone who has stopped resisting, or hear her out. Either choice costs something.

**Moves — after combat:**
- **The signal**: when the fight starts — the moment it becomes clear this ends in violence — she transmits. A pulse through the chip, brief and complete. The Fold knows Port Vala is gone. Act 2's opening changes accordingly.
- **The map**: Mira knows where the Fold is. If the crew kills her without getting it, they've lost the direct thread to Act 2. If they take her alive, or if she chooses to give it, she tells them plainly. She wants them to go. She thinks the Shepherd will change their minds.

*Description:* Slight, practical clothing, the kind that doesn't show wear. Moves through the church like someone who knows every corner of it. Nothing threatening in her manner — she's warm, attentive, unhurried. The wrong thing is harder to name than that. She was always going to be here when the crew arrived. She planned for this conversation.

**CONGREGATION MEMBERS (protecting Mira)**
*group, unarmed, devoted, in the way*

**Resilience**: 1
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: Nothing

**Instinct**: to put themselves between Mira and the threat

**Moves:**
- When Mira is threatened, move to stand between her and the crew — quietly, without aggression
- When the crew raises a weapon, don't flinch — they don't believe it will be used on them
- When someone goes down, the others don't scatter; they look at the crew, not at Mira
- When the fight has gone on long enough, more of them arrive from elsewhere in the building — confused at first, then drawn in by what they see

*Description:* The foreign workers, the contract labourers, the people the station forgot. The crew may recognise faces from the Burnt Market, from the corridors, from earlier conversations. These are not combatants. The crew knows this. That's what makes it hard.

**PAZ**
*solitary, unarmed, frightened, cornered*

**Resilience**: 1
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: The drug — always carrying product. Not a weapon. They use it themselves.

**Instinct**: to comply with whoever has power and keep moving

**Moves — in a confrontation:**
- **Go still**: when first approached by the crew, Paz stops moving entirely. Answers questions immediately and completely — not because they want to help, but because hesitating feels more dangerous than talking. They give up what's asked without being pushed. The problem is they don't know what they know until the right question is asked.
- **Run**: when physically threatened or when the situation becomes unpredictable, Paz runs. Not toward the Bears, not toward Mira — just away, into the tunnels or the deeper Burnt Market. Finding them again requires a move.
- **Warn Mira**: if Paz slips away from the crew and hasn't been fully flipped, their first instinct is to go to the church. They'll warn Mira that someone is asking questions. This advances Mira's threat clock and tells the crew exactly where Paz went.
- **Take the drug**: under extreme pressure — a real threat, a direct confrontation about Alexei — Paz takes the drug themselves. It doesn't make them helpful. It makes them distant, soft-edged, willing to say things they'd normally guard. What comes out may be more honest than anything they'd say sober. It may also be less coherent.
- **The name they didn't mean to give**: when the crew asks the right question about the headsets or the church's supplies, Paz gives up Mira's name before they've decided to. It comes out in the middle of a sentence about something else. They realise what they've said a beat after the crew does.

*Description:* Thin, careful in their movements, dressed for work rather than for being seen. Holds very still when frightened. The drug is always somewhere on them — a maintenance habit as much as an addiction. They carry the weight of Alexei's death in the way people carry things they've decided not to put down.

## Act 2: The Fold

*Full detail in `campaign/the-fold.md`.*

The Cult connection leads to a hidden station in the outer system — **the Fold**. A commune on the surface. Beneath it: a surgical factory manufacturing neural chips that integrate wearers into a hive network controlled by the Shepherd.

The Shepherd's endgame is expansion. Enough chipped minds distributed across the solar system to constitute an empire — one that grows without armies, without visibility, one node at a time.

The location of the Fold comes from Mira — either taken from her alive, or found in her personal effects. Without it, the crew has the network map from the ledger but no destination.

If Mira transmitted before she died, the Shepherd knows the crew is coming. If she didn't — if the crew moved fast enough — the Fold is not yet on alert. That difference shapes everything about how Act 2 opens.

The Shepherd is not an AI in a box. The Shepherd is an old person who went first, has been running an early version of the chip in their own skull for decades, and believes they are steering something that will end human loneliness.
