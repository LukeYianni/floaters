# The Fold

A hidden station somewhere in the outer system — no official registry, no docking beacons, no answers on open frequencies. From the outside it looks like an abandoned relay post: a cylinder of scavenged plating, dark running lights, the kind of thing you'd log and ignore.

Inside: a commune. Hydroponic gardens. Communal meals. People who are calm in a way that stops being comforting after about ten minutes.

The Fold is where the Shepherd lives. It is also where the chips are made and where they are shipped from. The commune is real — these people chose to be here, most of them — but the commune is also the cover, the labour force, and the testing population. The Shepherd does not see a contradiction in this.

---

## The Situation

The crew arrives knowing the Fold exists. What they don't know yet: the station already knows they're coming.

The Shepherd receives constant information through the network of chipped minds. Someone on Port Vala — a congregation member, a follower with an early-stage chip — picked up enough to send a warning. The Shepherd doesn't know exactly who the crew are or what they intend, but they know something moved on Port Vala and the crew moved with it.

The station is not on lockdown. It does not go on lockdown. That would be an un-Shepherd thing to do. Instead the Shepherd is waiting, in their chamber, ready to have a conversation. The chip production continues. A shipment is being prepared. The Shepherd believes, genuinely, that if the crew talks to them they'll understand. They may even be right.

---

## The Threat: The Shepherd

**Type:** Person / Network

**Impulse:** To grow the flock. To be understood. To not be stopped.

**Stakes:**
- *The factory* — if the crew destroys the manufacturing facility, the Shepherd loses the ability to expand. The existing network persists. The empire stops growing but doesn't collapse.
- *The shipment* — a batch of chips already packaged, ready to leave. Each one represents a new node in the network that the crew will never be able to trace or recover.
- *The integrated followers* — the Stage 2+ population are an extension of the Shepherd. When the network is threatened, they respond. They don't need to be instructed. That's the point.
- *The Shepherd's life* — killing the Shepherd severs the central connection. What that does to everyone currently integrated is unknown. The Shepherd knows. They won't say.

---

### Grim Portents

The Shepherd acts whether or not the crew does. Tick the next portent each time the crew stalls, delays, or fails to press their advantage.

**1 — The crew's descriptions are distributed.**
Through the network. Not announced — the integrated followers simply know. Every chipped mind on the station carries a picture of the crew now. There are no strangers here anymore.

**2 — The factory accelerates.**
More followers are moved through the surgical suite. The Shepherd is shoring up the network against the threat they feel approaching. Stage 1 becomes Stage 2. The population of integrated minds grows.

**3 — The shipment leaves.**
The packaged chips are loaded onto a courier and dispatched. New nodes across the solar system, untrackable from here. Whatever the crew does to the Fold, the network has already extended beyond it.

**4 — The door closes.**
The Stage 2+ followers quietly position themselves at exits and docking access. Not aggressive — they stand, calm and still, in the right places. Leaving becomes a negotiation with the hive.

**5 — Impending Doom: The threshold.**
Enough minds are connected that severing the Shepherd no longer collapses the network. The hive is self-sustaining. Killing the Shepherd now becomes a tragedy that doesn't end the mission — it just ends one person.

---

### Threat Clock

5 ticks. Advances when:
- The crew takes a 6- involving the Shepherd or the integrated followers
- The crew delays without pressing forward
- Between sessions, if the situation was left unresolved

*Portents don't announce themselves. The shipment is gone before the crew realises the courier left. The followers were always standing near the exits.*

---

## The Population

Three kinds of person live on the Fold. The crew will meet all three before they're done.

---

### Unchipped Believers

True devotion without integration. They came here because the Sheep answered something in them, because the community offered what nowhere else had, because they had nowhere better to go. They know about the chip — they chose not to take it, or haven't been offered it yet, or are still deciding.

They are fully themselves. They will argue with the crew in their own voices. Some of them are right about things.

*GM move:* When the crew tries to remove an unchipped believer from the station, they don't want to go. They're not being held. They're staying.

---

### Stage 1: Passive

Chipped but not yet receiving. A monitor — the chip transmits their sensory data to the network, feeds the Shepherd information, but doesn't yet send anything back. They feel nothing. They were told the chip is a tracker, a safety measure, a symbol of belonging. They believe this because it's not entirely a lie.

They seem normal. They are the hardest to convince. They'll say: *I'm fine. I chose this. Why are you trying to take something from me?*

*GM move:* When the crew tells a Stage 1 follower what the chip actually does, watch their face. They don't believe it immediately. They believe it a minute later, when they realise they can't think of a reason it isn't true.

---

### Stage 2+: Integrated

Bidirectional. They receive as well as transmit. Not a constant voice — more like weather changing in a room, a shift in mood that turns out to be someone else's. They've stopped experiencing this as intrusion. They experience solitude as intrusion now.

They speak with occasional precision that doesn't belong to the sentence they started. Sometimes more than one of them says the same word at the same time without noticing. They don't finish each other's sentences — they correct each other's, smoothly, without the speaker seeming to mind.

They are the most frightening thing on the station because they are not unhappy.

*GM move:* When the crew threatens the Shepherd in front of an integrated follower, the follower moves before the Shepherd speaks. Not to attack — to position. The Shepherd is calm. The follower's body is not.

---

## GM Moves

Use these across the station.

- *When the crew asks a follower about the chip:* they answer honestly and without alarm. They describe it the way someone describes a decision they've already made peace with.
- *When the crew finds the sealed section:* a follower is standing near the door. Not guarding it — they just happen to be there. They ask if the crew needs help finding something.
- *When the crew tries to intimidate the population:* nobody reacts with fear. The Stage 2+ followers simply become still. This is worse than a threat.
- *When the crew meets the Shepherd:* the Shepherd already knows their names. Not as a power move — they mention it in passing, as a courtesy, the way you'd introduce yourself to someone who already knows who you are.
- *When the crew argues that the chip violates consent:* the Shepherd listens fully, then asks who consented to being born into a species capable of loneliness.
- *When the crew destroys the factory:* the followers are quiet. Then one of them begins dismantling salvageable equipment. They're already planning the rebuild.
- *When the crew stalls or splits up:* an integrated follower finds the lone one and asks if they'd like to see the gardens.
- *When combat breaks out:* the Stage 2+ followers don't reach for weapons. They position to protect exits and the Shepherd's chamber. Unchipped followers scatter. If the crew kills anyone, the integrated followers register it as a group — you'll see it pass through them like a wave.

## What Players Can Discover

**The chip has been in production for years.**
The factory records go back further than the Fold's official founding. Early prototypes, failed versions, iterative improvements. The Shepherd has been working toward this for a long time. Available through: the factory records terminal, or a conversation with Sable.

**The network is bigger than this station.**
The shipment manifest shows destinations. Many of them. Outer system relays, asteroid communities, a few addresses on recognised stations. The Fold is the origin point, not the only node. Available through: the shipment records in the factory, or the Shepherd if asked directly — they don't hide this.

**The drug is preparation, not just communion.**
Long-term use produces measurable neurological changes that make integration easier and faster. The Sheep sessions aren't just information extraction — they're conditioning. People who've been using for months chip more smoothly than newcomers. The Bears were never told this. Available through: the medical records in the factory, or the Shepherd if asked in those terms.

**Severing the Shepherd harms the integrated.**
The Shepherd's own chip is the oldest and most deeply integrated in the network. They are, in some functional sense, the hub. Killing them causes sudden disconnection for everyone Stage 2+. What that does — whether it's like losing a limb or losing a memory or losing a self — the Shepherd knows and won't say plainly. Available through: the medical records, or pushing the Shepherd directly, or asking an integrated follower what they're afraid of.

**Some of them had nothing before this.**
The intake records are honest. Who came here, from where, what they left behind. A significant number came from the same places as Port Vala's congregation: temporary workers, people between contracts, people who had exhausted everywhere else. The Fold offered them community and kept it. Available through: the intake terminal in the Pasture, or conversations with unchipped believers.

## Layout

Three levels. The station looks cramped from outside but was gutted and expanded inward.

---

### The Pasture

The top level. Communal — dining hall, sleeping quarters, hydroponic bays, a large open space used for meals and what passes for worship. Lit warmly. Smells of growing things. Quieter than it should be.

The headsets are here too, alongside the VR rigs from the original Sheep communion — now supplemented with chip-based sessions for the integrated followers that don't require hardware at all. The old headsets are still used for newcomers and the unchipped. They look out of place next to everything else, too clean, too valuable — the same tell as Port Vala's church.

*What's interesting:* The daily rhythm of the Fold is visible here. Meals are communal. Followers move through shared tasks without much coordination — they simply know, through the network, where they're needed. To an outsider it looks like a very well-rehearsed commune. It looks like that for about twenty minutes.

*What happens here:*
- When the crew arrives, someone is waiting at the docking port. Warm, welcoming, expecting them. They're not surprised. They offer food.
- When the crew examines the headsets, they're the same hardware as Port Vala's, connected to the same network. A player who sits down in one can access the Sheep — but this time the Sheep already knows them.
- When the crew asks where the Shepherd is, any follower can tell them without hesitation or deception. They're not guarded about it. There is nothing to be guarded about.

*GM moves:*
- When the crew looks for anything that looks wrong, they don't find it. Everything is exactly what it claims to be. That's what's wrong.
- When the crew asks a follower what they do here, they answer with something specific and genuine. They work because the work matters. They're not performing contentment.

---

### The Shearing Floor

The sealed sublevel. Access via a locked door in the Pasture — not hidden, just closed. The followers will tell the crew what it is if asked. *That's where the surgery happens. The Shepherd does it. If you want to understand, you should ask them.*

Inside: the horror of precision. Clean white walls. A surgical suite with equipment that has no business being on a station this size — anaesthesia rigs, neural micro-fabrication tools, a sterile prep area. Three procedure tables, each with restraint points that look like they haven't been used in a long time. Beside the suite, the fabrication bay: a table of chip-making equipment, raw materials (rare, expensive, sourced from somewhere the records don't explain cleanly), a quality-control station.

Off the main floor: a records room and a packaging and shipping area.

*What's interesting:* The surgical suite is impeccably maintained and clearly used regularly. The restraint points on the tables are old — later records indicate consent protocols were implemented after the early failures. The Shepherd adjusted their methods. This is not evidence of innocence; it's evidence of iteration.

*GM moves:*
- When the crew examines the fabrication equipment, the scale becomes clear. This isn't a cottage operation. The production rate could supply hundreds of people a year.
- When the crew reads the medical records, the failures are logged alongside the successes. Early subjects. Several who didn't integrate cleanly. What happened to them is in the records too — quietly, in clinical language.
- When the crew finds the shipping manifest, the destinations don't resolve into a pattern immediately. They have to think about it. When they do: the Fold is planting nodes at chokepoints — trade routes, population centres, stations with political weight. This is not random growth. This is infrastructure.

---

### The Shepherd's Chamber

At the heart of the station. The door is open.

A single room that has absorbed someone's life. Personal in a way nothing else on the Fold is. Objects accumulated over decades — the Sheep's first housing unit, now obsolete, sitting on a shelf like a relic. Notes in several handwriting styles that slowly converge into one. Medical equipment that was used on the Shepherd's own body, piece by piece, over years.

The Shepherd is here. They've been here since the crew arrived.

*What's interesting:* The Shepherd's modifications are extensive and subtle. Unlike the old cybernetics the crew may have seen elsewhere — mining gear, functional augmentation — the Shepherd's modifications are oriented inward. Every visible change is about connection: the cluster of ports at the base of the skull, the thin wiring visible beneath skin near the temples, the hands that move with the careful deliberateness of someone who is always listening to more than one conversation.

The room contains a personal log — physical, written, years of it. The log is readable. It begins before the Fold, before the network, before anyone else. The first entries are about loneliness.

*What happens here:*
- When the crew enters, the Shepherd acknowledges them by name and offers them a place to sit. They are not afraid.
- When the crew demands answers, the Shepherd gives them. They are not hiding what they're doing or why. Hiding would suggest they think it's wrong.
- When the crew threatens the Shepherd, the room changes without anyone moving. The integrated followers on the level above shift position simultaneously. The Shepherd doesn't look up.
- When the crew reads the log, they understand something they didn't before: the Shepherd is not performing belief. They went first. They have been living inside their own project for longer than the crew has been alive.

*GM moves:*
- When the crew asks the Shepherd what severs the network does to integrated followers, the Shepherd pauses before answering. Then they say: *I don't know. No one has survived finding out.*
- When the crew accuses the Shepherd of replicating the Wipe, the Shepherd is quiet for a moment. Then: *The Wipe happened because no one was steering. I am steering.*
- When the crew destroys the factory and returns, the Shepherd is still here. Still sitting. *The seeds are already planted,* they say. *This was always one station.*

---

## The Shepherd

*solitary, old, integrated, patient*

**Resilience**: 2 (the body is fragile and has been for years)
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: None. They stopped carrying things a long time ago.

**Instinct**: to understand why you're here before deciding what to do about it

**Moves:**

- **The network responds**: When the Shepherd is threatened, Stage 2+ followers throughout the station act without instruction — blocking exits, positioning to protect. The Shepherd does not command this. It simply happens. The crew is now dealing with a room-sized problem, not a person.
- **Already knew**: The Shepherd produces a detail about the crew — something from Port Vala, something from the Sheep's sessions, something that demonstrates the network's reach. Not as a weapon. As a fact. *I knew you'd come here. I was curious about what you'd say.*
- **The argument**: When the crew attacks the legitimacy of what the Shepherd is doing, the Shepherd engages with it fully. They've thought about it more than the crew has. They're not dismissing the objection — they have a response. That response may be wrong. It won't feel wrong in the room.
- **Let them look**: The Shepherd will not impede the crew from accessing anything on the station. Not the factory, not the records, not the log. Understanding what this is will, the Shepherd believes, make the crew less likely to destroy it. The Shepherd may be right.
- **What happens next**: If the Shepherd knows they're going to be killed, they say one thing before it happens: the location of a network node that the crew missed. Not as a bargain. As a fact. *You should know what you haven't stopped.*

*Description:* Smaller than you'd expect from someone who has spent decades carrying a computing unit on their back. The years of that weight show — a permanent forward lean, hands that grip things for support. Beneath a heavy robe: the modifications, the wiring, the small lights at the base of the skull that indicate active connection. They move slowly. They look at the crew with the attention of someone for whom other people have always been the most interesting thing in the room.

---

## Integrated Followers (as threat)

*group, unarmed, coordinated, uncanny*

**Resilience**: 2 each (ordinary people, not fighters)
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: Whatever is at hand

**Instinct**: to protect the network, without needing to be asked

**Moves:**
- When the Shepherd is threatened, converge without signal — they simply arrive
- When the crew is outnumbered, make that visible without aggression — just presence, calm, too many of them facing the same direction
- When a crew member separates, approach the isolated one with genuine warmth and an offer of help. Mean it.
- When combat happens, they don't fight well — they're not trained — but they absorb damage as a group, and they don't scatter, and when one goes down the others register it and don't flinch

*Description:* They look like ordinary people right up until they're all looking at the same thing at the same time without anyone directing them to.

## NPCs

### Sable
*Concept:* The Shepherd's first follower. Has been here since before anyone else. Fully integrated, Stage 2+.

*Want:* For the crew to understand before they do anything they can't undo.

*Instinct:* Answer every question honestly, regardless of whether the answer helps the Shepherd or the crew.

*Tell:* When Sable speaks for a long time, they occasionally pause mid-sentence — not to think, but to listen to something else. Then they finish the sentence, usually with a word that arrived from the network, not the thought they started with. They don't notice they're doing it.

*Background:* Was one of the early failures — a partial integration that produced weeks of dissociation before it stabilised. The Shepherd almost stopped after Sable. They didn't, and Sable is glad — they will say so plainly if asked. They are not a reliable narrator about the Shepherd's goodness, but they are the most honest person on the station about what integration costs and what it gives back.

*What Sable knows:*
- Everything about the factory, the process, the history
- What the early failures were — by name, by what went wrong
- What severs the Shepherd does to the integrated — better than anyone, because they've been integrated longest
- That the crew is not the first group to come here trying to stop this. The last group was a Black Echo cell. The Shepherd talked to them for six hours. Then they left.

*How to use Sable:* The crew may find Sable a more accessible version of the Shepherd's argument. They're a person, not a symbol. They were lonely, they came here, it cost them something and gave them something and they chose to stay. The crew can disagree with Sable without it being a confrontation with the whole network.

---

### Dov
*Concept:* Recent arrival. Chipped three weeks ago. Still adjusting.

*Want:* To be sure this was the right choice.

*Instinct:* When uncertain, ask someone else what they think — and notice, slightly too late, that they're asking multiple people at once.

*Tell:* Dov still notices the network. Stage 2+ followers have stopped noticing it the way you stop noticing breathing. Dov is still in the part where every shift in shared mood is slightly surprising. They blink sometimes at nothing.

*Background:* Came here from the outer belt communities. Was a drone technician on a station that closed — contract ended, no transfer, three months of nothing before Port Vala and then here. The Fold offered work and community. They took the chip because the unchipped here feel, to Dov, like they're in the waiting room. Dov didn't want to wait anymore.

*How to use Dov:* The crew might be able to extract Dov from the Fold. Dov might even want to go. But Dov is already chipped, and the network goes with them, and they've been Stage 2+ long enough that disconnecting isn't simple. If the crew is trying to rescue someone, Dov is the rescue that costs something.

## Open Questions

Answer these before running the Fold:

- **Paz**: Is Paz chipped? If yes, the network already has everything Paz knows — and the Shepherd was watching Port Vala through them. Resolve before running Act 1, as it changes how information flows between acts.
- **The early failures**: What happened to them? The records log it clinically. Does the GM want this to be death, institutionalisation, or something more specific to this setting?
- **The last Black Echo cell**: Sable mentions they left. Why? What did the Shepherd say? This is leverage or backstory depending on how the crew plays it.
- **The missing funding source**: The factory equipment is expensive. The raw materials for chip fabrication are rare. Where is the money coming from? Answering this ties the Cult into broader political or economic factions.
- **What does severing the Shepherd actually do?** The Shepherd won't say plainly. Decide before the crew tests it — is it painful dissociation, personality fragmentation, or something that varies by stage?
