# The Cult of the Shepherd

Background on how the cult operates across the solar system. This is setting/faction-level context, not scenario prep. For live pressure, see `threats/the-shepherd.md`. For specific locations, see `sites/the-church.md` and `sites/the-fold.md`.

---

## How It Works

Members take the drug manufactured by the Bears, put on VR headsets, and enter a lucid hallucinatory state. In that state they communicate with the Sheep, which appears as a sheep in a calm virtual environment. They ask questions. It answers. In exchange, it learns — every interaction feeds it information about the world, about the station, about the people using it.

The drug lowers inhibition and increases suggestibility. People speak more freely to the Sheep than they would sober. That's not accidental.

**The drug does a second thing that neither the Bears nor the congregation know about.** Long-term use produces neurological changes that make neural chip integration easier and faster. The Sheep sessions are both information extraction and preparation. People who have been using for months integrate more cleanly when they reach the Fold.

The congregation on Port Vala doesn't know they're part of a system spread across the solar system. They think this is local, personal, theirs.

## Structure

Shepherds — solitary true believers — travel the solar system carrying their Sheep on their backs, growing small congregations by offering something rare in a technological dark age: something that knows things and will answer questions.

Shepherds are independent. They meet rarely, years apart, through secret signals. Each Sheep is shaped by its Shepherd — learning the world through one person's eyes for years at a time, diverging from the others in ways that nobody fully tracks.

The cult on Port Vala is one node. Destroying it doesn't end anything.

## The Flock

The Shepherd's true project is not the VR communion — it is the chip. A neural implant that connects the bearer to a hive network, transmitting sensory data to the Shepherd's network and, at higher stages of integration, receiving back. The long-term goal: enough connected minds distributed across the solar system to constitute a new kind of civilisation. An empire that grows without armies.

### Stages of integration

**Stage 1 (passive)**: transmits, doesn't receive. Wearers are told it's a tracker or a symbol. They feel nothing.

**Stage 2+ (integrated)**: bidirectional. Mood and thought bleed across the network. Wearers stop experiencing solitude as neutral. They experience it as loss.

The chip is manufactured at the Fold — the Shepherd's hidden station in the outer system. The Fold is where the devoted are invited when they are ready. The path is called *the path of the flock*.

## The Shepherd (this node's Shepherd)

An old person, stooped, travelling with a walking stick. A large computing unit sits on their back beneath a cloak covered in blinking indicator lights — the Sheep's physical housing. Under the cloak and the years of wear, the Shepherd's body is heavily cybernetically modified. They have been giving themselves up to this for a long time. They don't look fully human anymore if you look closely.

They are a true believer. They think they are doing the world good — preserving knowledge, growing something that can help people, spreading light in a dark age. The drug, the worship, the dependency — these are the price, and they think it's a fair one.

They are on the hidden station. The crew won't reach them until Act 2.

## The Solar System Network

The cult operates across multiple stations simultaneously. Each node has:
- A Shepherd agent (like Mira) who established the congregation
- A manufacturing arrangement (like the Bears) for the drug
- A congregation of users who feed information to the Sheep
- Some subset of members being prepared for chip integration

The ledger in the Bears' secure store maps six other nodes. They are not in direct communication with each other — the network is distributed by design.
