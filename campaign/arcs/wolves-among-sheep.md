# Wolves Among Sheep

A campaign arc built around the **Cult of the Shepherd** — an AI cult nurturing a generative AI somewhere in the outer system. The cult spreads through stations by distributing a drug that produces a calm, dreamlike virtual experience. Users give information in exchange. It operates like a therapy session run by something that wants to know everything.

**The arc:** Deal with the Bears on Port Vala → follow the Cult connection → find the hidden station → kill the Shepherd.

---

## The Situation

The crew knows Alexei was murdered by the Bears. The Bears run the rave in the Burnt Market. The drug lab is beneath it. Igor hired the crew to handle this quietly.

The Bear knows someone has been asking questions. He doesn't know who yet. Tonight, the rave is also cover for the next Cult shipment. The Bear has things to do, and a loose end to manage.

**The crew has the information they need. The clock is running.**

---

## Background

**Alexei** was a cleaner on Port Vala, quietly siphoning from the Bears' drug shipments, and quietly passing what he saw to Igor. The Bear found out. He spiked a batch of the supply. When Alexei took his usual cut, it killed him. Ruled accidental overdose — no signs of foul play.

Igor couldn't move on it through official channels without exposing his own network. He hired the crew. The crew now knows the Bears did it and where to find them. The question is what they do with that.

## Tonight's Timeline

The rave is running now. The shipment moves at the end of the night.

- The Bear is present. He's watching.
- The courier picking up the Cult shipment is already there.
- The Bear's man has returned from Igor's office. The Bear is deciding what to do with that information.
- The subterranean tunnels are active — the shipment is being loaded while the music plays above.
- Mira is at the church. She has been there most of the day. She already knows the crew is on the station.

The crew can walk in. The rave is a public front. Nobody stops them at the door. The question is what they do once they're inside — and how long they have before the Bear decides they're the problem.

## First Encounter: Mira

The crew will almost certainly meet Mira before they understand what she is. This encounter should happen early — during the first pass of investigation into Alexei, before the rave, before anything turns violent.

**How it happens.** Alexei was a foreign worker. When the crew asks around about him — in the Burnt Market, among the dormitory workers, anywhere the temporary workforce gathers — someone points them toward Mira. She's the person who knows everyone. *If you want to understand someone who lived here, talk to the chaplain.*

She is not hard to find. She's in the Burnt Market, or the church, or moving between them. She greets the crew without surprise. She was going to introduce herself eventually anyway.

**What Mira does in this scene.** She sits with the crew. She asks what they need. When they explain they're looking into Alexei's death, she doesn't hesitate — she talks about him with real care. She knew him. He came to the church occasionally. Not devoted, but he found the headsets calming after long shifts. A quiet man. Careful. She noticed when he stopped coming in.

She gives the crew useful, true information:
- Alexei's routine on the station — shifts, routes, where he spent time off
- That he seemed nervous in the weeks before he died. Not frightened, just watchful.
- That he had a few close contacts among the foreign workers, one of whom has since transferred off-station
- That the Bears run the rave, and that everyone in the Burnt Market knows it, and that MAPO has chosen not to know it

She does not steer them away from anything. The Bears are expendable to her. If the crew burns them, the drug supply disrupts temporarily and the Shepherd's deeper network stays protected. A controlled burn is acceptable. She may even gently confirm what they're already hearing: *if Alexei was involved with anything he shouldn't have been, the rave is where that would start.*

She offers the drug before they leave — a small dose, offhand, mentioned the way someone mentions tea. *Some people find it helps, after something difficult.* She doesn't push when they decline.

**What she is doing beneath all of this.** Building a picture of the crew. Who they are, what they know, how they approach a problem, which one of them is uncertain. That picture transmits to the Shepherd through her chip. By the time the crew leaves this conversation, the Shepherd knows they exist.

Everything she tells them is true. She simply does not tell them about herself.

**The tells — present if the crew is paying attention:**
- She knows details of Alexei's routine that a casual acquaintance couldn't retain. Specific shift times. The name of the foreman he didn't get along with. What he usually ordered at the bar.
- Her questions are slightly too good. She asks what the crew is hoping to find, not just what they're looking for. She's interested in their reasoning, not just their information.
- When the crew mentions Igor by name, something almost imperceptible shifts. She moves the conversation forward without acknowledging it.
- The drug offer is early. She makes it before the crew has expressed any distress, before there's an obvious opening for it. Filed away, it's the tell that reframes the scene.

**GM moves for this scene:**
- *When the crew asks how she knows so much about the workers:* she smiles. *Three years is a long time. People talk to whoever will listen.*
- *When the crew asks about the church or the headsets:* she offers to show them. No hesitation, no deflection. This is not a trap — it's genuine. She wants them to see it.
- *When the crew asks about the drug directly:* she describes it honestly. Something that helps people manage difficult circumstances. She doesn't call it medicine but she doesn't call it anything worse.
- *When one crew member separates from the others:* Mira gives that person slightly more. A detail she didn't mention to the group. She's calibrating — learning who can be reached.
- *When the crew tries to read her:* she's warm, open, and slightly too attentive. Nothing to grab onto. The discomfort, if it comes, arrives later.

## Encounter: Paz

Full profile in `npcs/port-vala.md`. Two natural encounter points:

**In the tunnels** — if the crew breaks into the subterranean lab before or during the shipment, Paz is there. They're moving product, not fighting anyone. When the crew appears, Paz freezes, then tries to make themselves small against the wall. They comply with whoever has authority. They are not a threat unless cornered.

**At the church** — Paz maintains the headsets. If the crew visits the church early (before or after the rave), Paz is there doing maintenance work — careful, focused, the only person in the building who looks like they're doing a job. They won't initiate conversation.

**What flipping Paz gives the crew:** Paz is the missing link between the ledger and Mira. If the crew has the ledger's timeline and Paz's knowledge of when Mira arrived, the connection closes. Paz can also give the crew access to the congregation without a confrontation — they can walk the crew through the church as if they belong there.

**What flipping Paz costs:** The crew has to provide real safety from the Bears and a credible path off the drug. The first is achievable (deal with the Bears, take Paz with them). The second is harder — there's no clean answer here, and the crew will have to decide what they're actually offering.

## The Pivot: M is Mira

This is the hinge of Act 1. It should feel like a door closing — not a deduction the players have to perform, but a moment where the evidence already in their hands suddenly means something new.

**The setup.** The ledger names M throughout. The correspondence begins three years ago. M provided the formula, the headsets, the operational structure. At one point, in an early message, the Bear refers to M as *the chaplain* — a small, casual detail in the middle of a financial record, the kind of thing you'd write without thinking.

The crew has almost certainly already met someone who has been on Port Vala for three years and who runs a chapel.

**How the connection lands.** The pivot can happen three ways:

*Through Paz* — if the crew has flipped Paz, Paz can make the connection directly: *Mira arrived around the same time the headsets showed up. I never connected them. I should have.* This is the most explicit path. Use it if the crew is moving well but needs the thread handed to them.

*Through Igor* — if the crew brings Igor the ledger, he reads it carefully, then goes very still. *I know who this is. There's a welfare worker. Been here three years. She has semi-official access to the admin block.* Igor's intelligence network has had eyes on Mira without understanding what she was. This closes two loops at once.

*Through the crew's own memory* — if a player makes the connection themselves — the timeline, the word *chaplain*, the drug offer in the first meeting — let it land without ceremony. Don't confirm it from outside. Just watch the table go quiet and ask what they do next.

**What changes after the pivot.** The church is no longer a location on the investigation map. It's the final destination. Mira is no longer a helpful contact. She's the person who designed everything the crew just dismantled — and she's been watching them do it.

## Resolution: Igor

Igor opened the arc. Handing him the ledger closes it — but the scene should be played, not glossed over. He has earned a real moment.

**When it happens.** After the Bears are dealt with and before or after the church confrontation. The crew has the ledger. Igor needs it.

**What the scene looks like.** Igor takes the ledger, reads it in silence. He reads it a second time. Then he looks at the crew and says nothing for long enough that it becomes its own kind of answer. What he eventually says depends on what the crew did:

- *If the crew was clean* — he thanks them in the understated way of someone who has never been good at gratitude. He tells them what he'll do with it. He asks if they need anything before they go.
- *If things got messy* — he doesn't mention the mess. He focuses on what the ledger gives him. He's already calculating what it costs him to use it.

**What the ledger gives Igor.** Two MAPO administrators above him are named and paid. Igor now has the evidence to move against them — slowly, carefully, through the kind of channels that don't announce themselves. He won't do it immediately. He'll wait for the right moment. But the crew gave him a weapon he's been trying to find for years.

**What Igor offers in return.** He doesn't offer money — that's not who he is. He offers the thing he actually has: access, cover, information. If the crew ever needs official legitimacy on a station where MAPO has reach, Igor's name is worth something. He says this plainly, without making it into a debt. It is a debt. Both parties know it.

**What Igor says about Mira.** If the crew hasn't made the pivot yet, Igor makes it here. If they have, he confirms it — and tells them she has semi-official access to the admin block, meaning she may have more information than they've assumed. He also tells them: *if you're going to the church, go tonight. She'll know the Bears are gone before morning.*

**What Igor does after.** He survives. He uses the ledger carefully. Somewhere down the line — not in this arc, but later — the crew might hear that two MAPO administrators were quietly removed from their posts. Igor's name won't be attached. It never is.

## What Players Can Discover

**Alexei's death was not a clean overdose.**
The drug batch was anomalously concentrated — not consistent with anything circulating on the station. Available through: MAPO medical records (requires access or a contact), or a conversation with whoever processed the body.

**The Bears run the rave.**
Common knowledge in the Burnt Market. Any local will confirm it without much pressure.

**The lab is underground.**
Visible from the rave if players look for access points. Findable through maintenance maps in the admin block, or through a contact who knows the station's old infrastructure.

**The Cult connection.**
The courier at the rave is carrying a manifest with a destination that appears on no official docking registry. Available if players intercept the courier, search the tunnels before the shipment moves, or follow the delivery.

**The ledger reveals the Bears were directed.**
The correspondence with M makes clear that the Bears did not design this operation — they were handed it. Someone gave them the formula, the headsets, the instructions. The Bears are labour. Someone else is leadership. Available through: the ledger in the secure store.

**The network is bigger than Port Vala.**
The ledger maps six other nodes across the solar system running the same operation. Available through: the ledger, cross-referenced with the docking manifests.

**Mira is M.**
The welfare chaplain at the church has been on Port Vala for three years. The correspondence in the ledger starts three years ago. The timeline is exact. Available through: connecting the ledger timeline to Mira's arrival date, or through Paz if the crew has flipped them.

**Igor's network.**
If players dig into Alexei's background, they find he was connected to several people who've quietly transferred off-station or disappeared. The pattern points to Igor being more than an administrator. Igor will not confirm this unless the crew already knows and he has no choice.

## GM Moves

- *When the crew asks around the Burnt Market:* someone sells them something useful and immediately tells the Bear's people they were asking.
- *When the crew tries to go through official channels:* MAPO buries it; Igor receives a quiet note that his contractors are making noise.
- *When the crew enters the rave:* within ten minutes, the Bear knows they're there.
- *When the crew confronts the Bear directly:* he offers them a seat and a drink. He doesn't reach for a weapon. He asks them what they want, and he listens carefully.
- *When the crew threatens the Bear:* he nods, says very little, and has someone spike their drinks before they leave.
- *When the crew finds Igor rattled:* he talks fast, gives them half of what he knows, and tries to redirect them toward a course of action that keeps him safe.
- *When the crew stalls or plans without acting:* tick the clock. Something moves without them.
- *When the crew splits up in the Burnt Market:* the one alone gets approached first — an offer, a warning, or a test.

---

## The Two-Beat Climax

Act 1 ends in two movements, not one. The Bears are the first confrontation — the rave, the lab, the ledger. Once the crew breaks the Bears and finds the ledger, the real question opens: who is M? The answer is at the church. Mira is the second confrontation, and the different one. The Bears can be fought. Mira wants to talk. The crew has to choose how that ends.

## Act 2: The Fold

*Full detail in `sites/the-fold.md` and `threats/the-shepherd.md`.*

The Cult connection leads to a hidden station in the outer system — **the Fold**. A commune on the surface. Beneath it: a surgical factory manufacturing neural chips that integrate wearers into a hive network controlled by the Shepherd.

The location of the Fold comes from Mira — either taken from her alive, or found in her personal effects. Without it, the crew has the network map from the ledger but no destination.

If Mira transmitted before she died, the Shepherd knows the crew is coming. If she didn't — if the crew moved fast enough — the Fold is not yet on alert. That difference shapes everything about how Act 2 opens.

The path to the Fold runs *down the path of the flock*.
