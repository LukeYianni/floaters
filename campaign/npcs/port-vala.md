# NPCs: Port Vala

Key characters for Act 1. Full scenario context in `arcs/wolves-among-sheep.md`.

---

## Mira

*Concept:* Welfare chaplain and community support worker. Has been on Port Vala for three years, serving the foreign workforce — the people nobody else looks after. Also the Shepherd's agent who established this node: brought the drug formula to the Bears, supplied the headsets, built the congregation from nothing, and has been transmitting everything she learns back to the Fold ever since.

*Want:* For the crew to understand what they'd be destroying before they decide to destroy it.

*Instinct:* Move toward the person who is most uncertain. They're the one worth talking to.

*Tell:* She knows too much. Names, histories, patterns — details that couldn't survive two conversations without a perfect memory or a running record. She's always present before she's been called. The congregation trusts her completely, and that trust is not misplaced — she has genuinely helped every one of them. That's not the part she's hiding.

*Background:* Arrived on Port Vala three years ago carrying the Shepherd's commission and the cover of a genuine vocation. The welfare work is real — she cares about the people here, and they can feel it. The Bears were a means: she approached them with a business arrangement that required nothing they weren't already willing to do. Formula, infrastructure, distribution. The headsets came later, sourced through the Shepherd's supply chain, routed through three intermediaries. Her chip has been active for two years. Stage 1+ — she transmits constantly and receives impressions: moods, fragments, the sense of a larger network at the edge of her awareness. She does not experience this as surveillance. She experiences it as not being alone.

*What Mira knows:*
- The drug formula, its source, and its secondary neurological function
- The Bears' full operation — she designed their involvement
- Every congregation member on Port Vala, in detail
- The Fold's location, and how to reach it
- That the Bears are one of many manufacturing nodes across the solar system
- That the crew has been investigating since they arrived — she has known since the second day

*What Mira will do:* She will not run. She will not reach for a weapon first. If the crew confronts her, she will sit down, offer the drug, and try to have the conversation.

*If captured rather than killed:* Mira will tell the crew where the Fold is. Not as a bargain. She wants them to go. She believes the Shepherd will change their minds.

---

### Mira (stat block)

*solitary, chipped, patient, dangerous-by-proxy*

**Resilience**: 2 (she is not a fighter and has never needed to be)
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: The drug — she carries a dose. She will offer it before she does anything else.

**Instinct**: to make you understand before you act

**Moves — before violence:**
- **The congregation moves**: when Mira is threatened inside the church, devoted members step between her and the crew without being asked — not to fight, just to be present. The crew has to decide what to do with that.
- **She already knows**: Mira produces a detail about the crew — something she shouldn't have — quietly, without making it a threat.
- **The offer**: before any confrontation escalates, Mira offers the drug and a headset. *Before you decide, understand what you'd be ending.* She genuinely believes the experience changes the calculation. She may be right.

**Moves — during combat:**
- **Dose the room**: the church is enclosed, the ventilation is poor. She breaks a vial — not thrown, just opened and set down — and steps back. The drug disperses slowly. One crew member finds their certainty softening. The GM picks whoever is most vulnerable in the fiction. They act at disadvantage until they get clear air.
- **Speak to the uncertain one**: in the middle of the fight, Mira stops engaging with the room and addresses one crew member directly. By name. With a specific detail from weeks of transmissions. A conversation, conducted while the fight continues around them.
- **The congregation absorbs it**: when a blow would reach Mira, a congregation member takes it instead. They didn't plan it — they just moved. The crew has now hurt someone who came here for help.
- **More of them come**: if the fight extends, congregation members arrive from deeper in the church. The numbers shift. The crew's margin shrinks.
- **She stops**: at some point Mira sits down. In the middle of the fight. She doesn't reach for anything. She looks at whoever is nearest and says: *If you need to, then do it. But I'd rather you understood first.*

**Moves — after combat:**
- **The signal**: the moment it becomes clear this ends in violence — she transmits. The Fold knows Port Vala is gone. See `threats/the-shepherd.md`.
- **The map**: Mira knows where the Fold is. If the crew kills her without getting it, they've lost the direct thread to Act 2.

## Paz

*Concept:* The Bears' drug runner for the cult. Addicted to the product they distribute. Coerced into killing Alexei and handed his job as the price of their own survival.

*Want:* To get through each day without having to think about what they did.

*Instinct:* Comply with whoever has power over them. Keep moving. Don't stop long enough to feel it.

*Tell:* Paz holds themselves very still when they're frightened, like staying small might help. They answer questions quickly and completely — not because they want to help, but because hesitating feels more dangerous than talking.

*Background:* Paz was a congregation member before they were a runner. They knew Alexei. The Bears identified them as useful — already embedded in the church, already dependent on the drug — and coerced them into killing him. Now they maintain the headsets, manage the dwindling supply, and keep the church running through desperation rather than competence. They are trapped in every direction.

*What Paz knows:*
- The Bears' supply chain — where the drug comes from, how it moves, who handles it
- The church — every member, every satellite congregation across the station
- Alexei — what he was like, what his routine was, that his death didn't feel right
- The headsets — where they came from, how they connect to the hidden station
- That there is a hidden station, though not where it is
- That Mira arrived around the same time the headsets did (doesn't consciously connect this — but will once the crew asks the right question)

*How to flip Paz:* They don't want to be doing this. The crew needs to offer two things: safety from the Bears, and something that addresses the dependency. Offer one without the other and Paz can't take it.

---

### Paz (stat block)

*solitary, unarmed, frightened, cornered*

**Resilience**: 1
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: The drug — always carrying product. Not a weapon. They use it themselves.

**Instinct**: to comply with whoever has power and keep moving

**Moves:**
- **Go still**: when first approached, stops moving entirely. Answers questions immediately and completely — fear, not cooperation.
- **Run**: when physically threatened or when the situation becomes unpredictable. Not toward the Bears, not toward Mira — just away. Finding them again requires a move.
- **Warn Mira**: if Paz slips away without being flipped, first instinct is the church. Advances Mira's threat clock.
- **Take the drug**: under extreme pressure, Paz takes the drug themselves. Makes them distant, soft-edged, willing to say things they'd normally guard. What comes out may be more honest than anything they'd say sober.
- **The name they didn't mean to give**: when the crew asks the right question about the headsets or the church's supplies, Paz gives up Mira's name before they've decided to. It comes out in the middle of a sentence about something else.

## The Bear

*Concept:* The actual head of the Bears operation on Port Vala. Forgettable by design. Has been in this business long enough that he doesn't need to look like what he is.

*Want:* Operational continuity. No visibility. No loose ends.

*Instinct:* to never be the one holding the weapon when anyone's watching

*Tell:* He moves like someone who's done this before and didn't enjoy it. When he's deciding whether you're a problem, he listens very carefully and says almost nothing.

---

### The Bear (stat block)

*solitary, trained, cautious, ruthless*

**Resilience**: 4
**Armor**: 1 (concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistol (1 damage, nearby), combat knife (1 damage, next to)

**Instinct**: to never be the one holding the weapon when anyone's watching

**Moves:**
- **One clean shot**: The Bear doesn't draw until the moment is exactly right. When he fires, it's from cover or concealment, at whoever is most exposed.
- **Exploit the wound**: when a crew member is already hurt or distracted, the Bear moves to them first. Not to finish the fight — to create the opening he needs to leave.
- **The knife is quieter**: when he's close enough that a shot would draw attention, he uses the knife instead. Fast, minimal, no announcement.
- **Controlled exit**: when the Bear decides to go, he creates one problem on the way out — overturns something, puts a body between himself and pursuit, fires once without looking. Then he's through the door.
- **Already gone**: when the Bear reaches an exit, he doesn't linger. By the time the crew reacts, there's nothing to chase.

## Orel

*Concept:* The Bear's visible face on the floor of the rave. A performer. The point is that everyone can see him — that's the cover.

*Want:* To be in charge of the room.

*Instinct:* to make the problem visible so everyone can watch him solve it

*Tell:* The performance is practiced enough that it's almost real. In a fight, the performance becomes the weapon.

---

### Orel (stat block)

*solitary, armed, brutal, organized*

**Resilience**: 7
**Armor**: 2 (size and a concealed vest)
**Equipment**: Pistol (1 damage, nearby), his hands

**Instinct**: to make the problem visible so everyone can watch him solve it

**Moves:**
- **Command the room**: when Orel raises his voice and points at someone, the moment freezes. Anyone who acts through that moment acts at disadvantage. His people use it.
- **The setup**: Orel telegraphs something enormous — a shout, a big swing, a step forward. It's bait. When someone reacts, he does something else entirely: a grab, a headbutt, a throw into the bar.
- **Bearlike grip**: when Orel gets hold of someone, they're his problem until someone pulls him off.
- **Take it**: when Orel takes a hit without going down, he smiles. He keeps moving forward. Part of the performance.
- **Make an example**: when Orel puts someone down dramatically and visibly, the crew loses their initiative. He's in charge of this room.

## Bears (squad)

*squad, armed, trained, organized*

**Resilience**: 2 each
**Armor**: 0 (dressed as staff — nothing visible)
**Equipment**: Concealed pistols (1 damage, nearby), batons (1 damage, next to, non-lethal)

**Instinct**: to protect the operation without making a scene

**Moves:**
- When Orel signals, converge on the problem efficiently and without drama
- When someone gets too close to staff-only areas, redirect firmly and radio it in immediately
- When violence breaks out in the rave, use the civilian panic as cover and confusion
- When one of them goes down, the others tighten up rather than scatter
- When the crew is outnumbered, make that fact visible without saying a word

## Congregation Members (protecting Mira)

*group, unarmed, devoted, in the way*

**Resilience**: 1 each
**Armor**: 0
**Equipment**: Nothing

**Instinct**: to put themselves between Mira and the threat

**Moves:**
- When Mira is threatened, move to stand between her and the crew — quietly, without aggression
- When the crew raises a weapon, don't flinch — they don't believe it will be used on them
- When someone goes down, the others don't scatter; they look at the crew, not at Mira
- When the fight has gone on long enough, more of them arrive from elsewhere in the building

*Note:* The crew may recognise faces from the Burnt Market, from earlier conversations. These are not combatants. The crew knows this. That's what makes it hard.

## Igor

*Concept:* MAPO mid-level administrator with a quiet intelligence operation on the side. Alexei worked for him. He hired the crew because official channels would expose his own network.

*Want:* The ledger. What it contains gives him leverage he's been trying to build for years.

*Instinct:* to give you half of what he knows and see what you do with it

*Tell:* When Igor is rattled, he talks faster and redirects — moves the conversation toward a course of action that keeps him safe. When he's in control, he goes very still.

*What Igor can offer:* Official cover on stations where MAPO has reach. He won't offer money — that's not who he is. He offers the thing he actually has.

*What happens to Igor after:* He survives. He uses the ledger carefully. The crew may eventually hear that two MAPO administrators were quietly removed. Igor's name won't be attached. It never is.
