# Design Principles

## Core Fantasy

A crew of specialists builds momentum together and unleashes it in decisive moments.

- “I’ve got this” (Edge)
- “We’ve got this” (Flow)

---

## Key Goals

### 1. Team-First Play
- Mechanics should reinforce crew coordination
- Individual success feeds team success

---

### 2. Specialisation Drives Play
- Each playstyle has unique, reliable ways to generate Edge
- Players are rewarded for “doing their thing”

---

### 3. Action Over Planning
- Acting decisively is rewarded
- Overplanning is discouraged

---

### 4. Risk is the Engine
- Taking risks generates resources
- Safe play does not

---

### 5. Forward Momentum
- Players are encouraged to push when things go wrong
- Failure should not stall the game

---

### 6. Minimal Bookkeeping
- Small caps (Edge 0–2, Flow 0–6)
- No complex modifiers or tracking

---

### 7. Shared Resource Without Friction
- Flow is powerful but not used for minor bonuses
- Avoids players competing over small spends

---

### 8. No Downtime Dependency
- Core resources (Edge, Flow) are generated during action
- No reliance on downtime to restore or generate Edge or Flow
- Downtime activities (Training, healing) are supplemental, not required for advancement

---

### 9. Meaningful Decisions
Players constantly choose between:
- Personal gain (Edge)
- Team investment (Flow)

---

### 10. Fiction-First Mechanics
- Resource generation requires fictional justification
- Mechanics follow narrative positioning
