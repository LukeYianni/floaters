# Stonetop — Information Gathering Moves

Source: Moves & Gear.pdf, Character Playbooks.pdf

---

## Core Moves (available to all characters)

### SEEK INSIGHT
When you **study a situation or person**, looking to the GM for insight, roll +WIS.
- **10+**: ask the GM 3 questions from the list below
- **7-9**: ask 1
- **Either way**: gain advantage on your next move that acts on the answers

Questions:
- What happened here?
- What is about to happen?
- What should I be on the lookout for?
- What here is useful or valuable to me?
- Who or what is really in control here?
- What here is not what it appears to be?

### KNOW THINGS
When you **consult your accumulated knowledge**, roll +INT.
- **10+**: the GM will tell you something interesting and useful about the topic at hand
- **7-9**: the GM will tell you something interesting — it's on you to make it useful
- **Either way**: the GM might ask, "How do you know this?"

## Playbook Moves

### The Fox

**THE NATURAL** (Background move)
When you Seek Insight, you may roll +INT instead of +WIS. Also adds "What opportunity does no one else see?" to the question list.

**DANGER SENSE**
You can always ask the GM, "Is there an ambush or trap here?" If they say "yes":
- **10+**: ask the GM both questions below
- **7-9**: ask 1
- **Either way**: gain advantage on your next roll to act on the answers

Questions: What will trigger the ambush or trap? / What will happen once it's triggered?

On a 6-, don't mark XP; you know there's a trap or ambush, but nothing bad happens just yet.

**PERCEPTIVE**
When you Seek Insight, you may ask 1 additional question. Even on a 6-, you can ask 1 question (though you might not like how you learn the answer).

**UNDER YOUR SKIN**
When you **engage an NPC in conversation**, you can ask the GM 1 honest answer from:
- What are they expecting me to do?
- What, in general, are they trying to hide?
- What do they want to happen?

---

### The Heavy

**FROSTY**
When you **Defy Danger by keeping calm and carrying on**, on a 10+ you can also ask the GM a question you could ask when Seeking Insight. You have advantage on your next roll to act on the answer.

**SITUATIONAL AWARENESS** (Requires level 6+ and Musclebound)
When you Seek Insight, add the following to the list of questions you can ask:
- Who or what here is the biggest threat?
- What is my enemy's true position?
- What here can I use as a weapon?

When a fight breaks out, ask the GM 1 question you could ask when Seeking Insight.

---

### The Judge

**LEGACY** (Background move)
When you **Know Things about the people or history of Stonetop**, you have advantage.

**HOUND OF ARATIS**
When you Seek Insight, you can always ask "What here is tainted by chaos?" for free, even on a 6-.

**TRUTH OR CONSEQUENCES**
When you **look into someone's eyes and gaze upon their soul**, you can ask their player, "Are you lying or hiding something from me?" and get an honest answer. If the answer is "Yes," you have advantage on your next roll against them.

When you **lie or otherwise deceive someone through words**, they have disadvantage on their next roll against them.

**VISION UNCLOUDED**
When you Seek Insight, you can always ask "What here is hidden by illusion or magic?" for free, even on a 6-.

**WELL-READ**
When you **name the source in which you read about the matter at hand**, roll +WIS to Know Things instead of +INT.

**KNOWLEDGE IS POWER**
When you **roll 10+ to Know Things**, you or an ally gain advantage on the next roll to act on what you learn.

### The Lightbearer

**ALL IS ILLUMINATED**
When you **look closely on another and see their soul laid bare**, roll +WIS:
- **10+**: ask their player 1 question from the list, plus "And what would make them feel loved, beautiful, or worthy?"
- **7-9**: ask their player 1 question from the list
- **Either way**: they must answer truthfully

Questions:
- Of what are they most ashamed?
- What do they most desire or covet?
- What hope have they abandoned?
- Who or what is most precious to them?

**HELIOR'S UNBLINKING EYE**
When you **stare into the sun long enough to lose your vision**, name a person or place that you know and roll +WIS:
- **10+**: briefly glimpse your subject as if from a great height, and choose 2 from the list below
- **7-9**: briefly glimpse your subject as if from a great height, and choose 1

Options: The glimpse lasts as long as you wish / Your point of view shifts to very close range / You recover your vision quickly

### The Blessed

**SPIRIT TONGUE** (Starting move)
You can speak with beasts and spirits. You can always ask the GM, "What spirits are active here?" and get an honest answer.

**CALL THE SPIRITS** (Requires Spirit Tongue)
When you **spend 1 Stock and perform a short rite**, the spirit(s) of a place or object manifest and hear you out. What they do next is up to them.

---

### The Marshal

**READ THE LAND**
When you **first take a moment to survey the terrain**, ask the GM one of the following; gain advantage on your next roll to act on the answer:
- What's the best way in, out, through, or past?
- Where's the best spot for a trap or an ambush?
- Where's the most defensible position?
- What here is out of place?

**TAKE THE MEASURE**
When you **size someone up**, ask their player one of the following and get an honest answer:
- Can I trust them to (___)?
- What do they intend to do?
- What weakness of theirs can I exploit?

If they **fear or respect you** (their call), you can ask another question. You can't use this move on them again until your relationship changes significantly.

**LIKE AN OPEN BOOK** (Requires level 6+ and Take the Measure)
When you Take the Measure of someone who fears or respects you, your second question can be anything you want. The GM might ask how you could possibly know this; tell them or ask something else.

### The Ranger

**WIDE WANDERER** (Background move)
When you **Know Things about the wider world**, you can roll +WIS instead of +INT.

**EXPERT TRACKER** (Starting move)
When you **Seek Insight by searching for or studying the signs left by passing creatures**, you can ask "What happened here recently?" for free, even on a 6-.

When you **follow a creature's trail**, roll +WIS:
- **7+**: follow it to a significant change in terrain or activity
- **10+**: also ask the GM a reasonable question about your quarry and get a useful answer

**NATURALIST**
When you **Know Things about beasts, natural environs, or spirits of the wild**, you have advantage.

**PREDATOR**
When you Seek Insight, add the following to the list of questions you can ask:
- Who or what here is the easiest prey?
- How is ___ weak or vulnerable?

When **acting on the answer to either question**, deal an extra 1d4 damage.

**SNIFF OUT CORRUPTION**
When you Seek Insight, you can ask, "What here stinks of the unnatural?" for free, even on a 6-.

**WARDEN OF THE WILD**
When you **defeat a perversion of nature**, you can ask the GM 2 of the following and get a useful answer:
- Will it come back? If so, how can I stop it?
- Will its taint spread? If so, how can I contain it?
- What useful (but gristly) bits can I harvest?
- What else can I learn about it or its ilk?

### The Seeker

**ATTUNED** (Starting move)
When you Seek Insight, you can always ask, "What here is infused with magic?" for free, even on a 6-.

**LET'S MAKE A DEAL** (Starting move)
When you Seek Insight, add "What do they really want or need?" to the list of questions. When you **Persuade by offering them something that you know they want or need**, treat a 7-9 as a 10+.

**COUNTERMEASURES**
When you **witness a magical effect**, you may ask the GM, "how can this be countered or interrupted?" and get an honest answer. You or an ally gain advantage on your next roll to act on the answer.

**EVERYTHING BURNS**
When you **inspect a work of artifice or magic for a fatal flaw**, roll +INT:
- **7+**: the GM will reveal the best way to destroy/sabotage it
- **10+**: you or an ally also gain advantage to act on the info

**LOGBOOK** (2 uses, slow)
When you (and only you) **consult your logbook and expend a use**, you can ignore a Know Things roll you just made and treat the result as a 10+. Resets each Seasons Change.

**WELL VERSED** (Starting move, 3 topics)
When you **Know Things about one of your topics**, you can ask the GM a follow-up question of your choice (even on a 6-).

**NEVER AT A LOSS**
When you Know Things and roll a 6-, you may choose not to mark XP. If you don't, the GM tells you nothing interesting or useful — instead tells you how you could learn more.

**CRYPTOLOGIST** (Requires Polyglot)
When you **study encoded, forgotten, or arcane marks or writing**, roll +INT:
- **10+**: fully decipher it
- **7-9**: get the gist in a few minutes, but full deciphering will take an hour or so

**DEEP INSIGHT** (Requires level 6+ and Attuned)
When you **Seek Insight about something magical**, you may ask 1 additional question, not limited to the list. Even on a 6-, you get to ask this question.

### The Would-be Hero

**INQUIRING MINDS**
When you **seek out and receive honest advice**, gain advantage on your next roll to follow that advice.

**RESOURCEFUL**
When you **Defy Danger and roll a 6-**, ask the GM a question from Seek Insight after they describe what happens. Gain advantage on your next roll to act on the answer.

**VOICE OF EXPERIENCE** (Requires level 6+ and Inquiring Minds)
When **another PC asks you for advice and tells you what they think is best**, they have advantage on their first roll to follow your advice.

Also: when you Seek Insight, you can always ask, "What is about to happen?" for free, even on a 6-.

## Design Patterns

A few structural patterns worth noting:

**The standard Seek Insight chassis**: roll a stat, ask N questions from a fixed list on 10+, ask fewer on 7-9, gain advantage on your next move if you act on the answers. Playbook moves extend this chassis by: adding questions to the list, adding a free question on any result (even 6-), changing which stat you roll, or asking extra questions.

**Honest answers vs. free information**: Many moves require the GM or another player to answer "honestly" — this is distinct from the GM choosing what information to volunteer. It puts truth-extraction in the players' hands.

**Specialised Seek Insight by domain**: Each playbook's info-gathering moves are locked to their fiction. The Ranger reads tracks and nature; the Seeker reads magic; the Judge reads chaos and deception; the Fox reads people and opportunity; the Marshal reads terrain and enemies. Each is a narrowing of the base move into a specific expertise.

**The 6- safety valve**: Several moves (Danger Sense, Expert Tracker, Attuned, Sniff Out Corruption, Hound of Aratis) grant a free question even on a 6-. This ensures a specialist never goes entirely blind in their domain, even when things go wrong.

**Hold-and-spend variants**: Take the Measure (Marshal) asks one question now but gates a follow-up behind a fictional condition (fear/respect). This models social dynamics differently from a straight roll.
