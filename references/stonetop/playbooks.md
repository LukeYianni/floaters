# Stonetop — Playbooks Reference

Nine playbooks: The Blessed, The Fox, The Heavy, The Judge, The Lightbearer, The Marshal, The Ranger, The Seeker, The Would-be Hero.

---

## Structure of each playbook

Each playbook has:
- **Background** (choose 1): a starting situation that grants specific starting moves
- **Instinct** (choose 1): a named behavioral drive in the format "To [infinitive]"
- **Moves**: a starting set plus optional picks; cross-playbook moves available at higher levels
- **Special possessions** (pick 2): starting gear
- **Appearance**, **Place of origin and name**: fiction-grounding choices

Instincts appear on every playbook. Each has 5–6 options specific to that playbook's flavor plus a blank. The same Instinct name can appear on multiple playbooks (e.g. FREEDOM appears on The Fox; CONSCIENCE on The Fox and elsewhere).

## Hard gates vs. soft advantages

**Hard gates** — things only this playbook can do, because they require extraordinary fictional permission:

| Move | Playbook | What it gates |
|---|---|---|
| Spirit Tongue | Blessed | Speaking with beasts and spirits |
| Animal Companion | Ranger | Bonding with and acting through a beast |
| Wild Speech | Ranger | Understanding and communicating with beasts |
| Arcana system | Seeker | Wielding major and minor arcana (magical artifacts) |
| Invoke the Sun God | Lightbearer | Channelling divine Invocations |
| Danu's Grasp | Blessed | Binding spirits and perversions of nature magically |
| Crew | Marshal | Having a trained militia crew |

**Soft advantages** — anyone can attempt these, but this playbook has specific named moves that fire off their particular method:

| Playbook | Domain | How it works |
|---|---|---|
| Fox | Stealth, deception, theft | Catlike, Burgle, Light Fingers — fictional triggers reward doing it in Fox's specific style |
| Heavy | Brutal close combat | Battle Joy (triggers on *losing yourself* in battle), Hard to Kill, Dangerous |
| Marshal | Leadership, logistics | Logistics (advantage on Muster/Pull Together), Stentorian, Front Line Leader |
| Judge | Authority, law, truth | Bear Witness, Censure, Truth or Consequences — moves that fire off the Judge's specific flavor of authority |
| Ranger | Tracking, wilderness, ranged | Expert Tracker, Naturalist, Mental Map, Stalker — anyone can track, Ranger has specific moves that produce richer outputs |
| Would-be Hero | Heroic risk-taking | Anger is a Gift, Potential for Greatness, Underestimated — moves that fire off their particular emotional/narrative texture |

## The move structure pattern

Stonetop moves follow a consistent structure:

> **When you [specific fictional trigger]**, [mechanical outcome with options].

The trigger is always fictional — it asks something of the fiction, not just "when you roll to do X." The outcome is specific: a list of options to pick from, a stat roll with differentiated results, an automatic benefit if the fiction holds.

Moves that give generic "+1d" or "+1 damage" exist but are the minority and tend to be lower-tier. Signature playbook moves are always tied to specific fictional conditions.

**Crew-feeding** is a common pattern: "the first ally to act on your answer gains 1 Edge," "an ally gains advantage on their next roll," "each ally holds 1 Inspiration." Individual excellence creates crew opportunity.

---

## Cross-playbook moves

Each playbook has one or more moves that allow taking moves from other playbooks:
- **Dabbler** (Fox, level 2+): take a move from Heavy, Marshal, or Seeker playbooks
- **Seasoned Warrior** (Heavy, level 2+): take from Fox, Marshal, or Seeker
- **Arts of War** (Marshal, level 2+): take from Fox, Heavy, Judge, or Ranger
- **Worldly** (Ranger, level 2+): take from Blessed, Fox, Heavy, or Marshal
- **Wild Soul** (Blessed, level 2+): take a Ranger move
- **Versatile** (Would-be Hero, level 2+): take from any playbook

These are the explicit breadth mechanism. Depth (your own playbook) is free; breadth (other playbooks) costs a specific move slot and is level-gated.
