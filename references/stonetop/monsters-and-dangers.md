# Stonetop: Monsters and Dangers

Summary of Stonetop's approach to dangers, monsters, and combat. Use this as a reference when designing Floaters equivalents — don't re-read the PDF.

---

## Two types of danger

**Hazards** — environmental dangers. Can't be fought; must be avoided, endured, or overcome. No initiative, no stats. The GM introduces them as fiction and makes moves with them.

**Monsters** — active dangers with intent. Things the PCs can fight (or fight off). Include people, beasts, spirits, constructs — anything that opposes the PCs physically.

---

## Monster format

Each monster has:

1. **Name** — what it's called, at least in your notes
2. **Tags** — descriptive words that define how it hunts, fights, and behaves. See below.
3. **HP** — how much damage before it's out of action
4. **Armor** — how well protected it is; damage is reduced by armor
5. **Damage** — what it deals on a hit, with attack tags
6. **Special qualities** *(optional)* — traits not covered by tags (e.g. "immune to fire", "echolocation")
7. **Instinct** — "to [do something]." What the monster does by default, unprompted. Use it when you're not sure what it does next.
8. **Moves** — 3–5 specific GM moves. Written to complete: "the monster can/will..."
9. **Description** — appearance, habitat, behavior. Write for yourself, not for reading aloud.
10. **Optional elements** — lore, player-facing custom moves, tactics as if/then statements

## Tags

### Organization (pick 1)
| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *solitary* | Fights alone. 12 HP base. |
| *group* | 2–5 together. 6 HP each. |
| *horde* | 6 or more. 3 HP each. |

### Size (pick 1)
| Tag | HP modifier |
|---|---|
| *tiny* | −2 HP |
| *small* | — |
| (adult human-sized) | — |
| *large* | +4 HP |
| *huge* | +8 HP |

### Nature (add all that apply)
*spirit, Fae, construct, undead, corrupted, primordial*

### Behavior (add all that apply)
*stealthy, cautious, terrifying, organized, cunning, devious, hoarder, magical*

## Attack tags

Range tags *(in italics)*:
- *hand* — tight quarters, up close
- *close* — melee, 1–2 steps
- *reach* — 3–4 steps
- *near* — up to ~30 steps
- *far* — up to ~100 steps

Effect tags:
- *area* — hits everything in a zone
- *forceful* — knocks foes around/down
- *grabby* — grapples or restrains
- *messy* — especially destructive, tears things apart
- *[n] piercing* — ignores n points of armor
- *ignores armor* — armor is 0

Damage modifiers:
- Impressively strong: +2 damage, *forceful*
- Strikes precisely: +1 piercing
- Physical injury not the worst danger: −1 die size
- Abhors violence: disadvantage on damage

## Armor baseline

| Protection | Armor |
|---|---|
| Cloth and flesh | 0 |
| Thick hide, leather | 1 |
| Mail, scale | 2 |
| Steel, bony plates | 3 |
| Magical/supernatural | 4 |

Additional +1 armor if: tiny, bears a shield, skilled in defense, lacks vital organs.

---

## HP modifiers (beyond base)

+4 if particularly tough/durable  
+2 if smiled upon by the fates  
+4 if animated by more than biology  
+3 if it lacks vital organs

## Instinct

Write as "to [verb]." It should describe a *pattern of behavior*, not a specific action.

- Good: "to covet", "to protect its master", "to ensnare"
- Too specific (more like a move): "to swallow prey whole"
- Too vague: "to hunt"

Use the instinct when you don't know what the monster does next.

---

## Moves

Write 3–5 moves per monster. Each one finishes the sentence "the monster can..."

**Write moves that:**
- Reflect how it hunts or fights specifically (not just "attack")
- Show its deceptive/sneaky tricks (if it has them)
- Describe how it escalates or gets worse
- Show how it defends itself or retreats
- Reflect any special form of attack

**Avoid:**
- Moves that just restate the attack stat ("stab with a spear")
- Moves that are just "do its instinct"
- Vague moves that give you no direction ("bully and manipulate people")

---

## Running monsters at the table

- Portray monsters as *monsters* — scary, dangerous, motivated. Not fair encounters.
- Every monster has an instinct and self-preservation. Many can be scared off, bargained with, or reasoned with.
- **Let things burn.** Don't protect your monsters. If the fiction and the dice point toward injury or death, go with it.
- Introduce monsters as a GM move: frame a scene, put the monster in it, ask "what do you do?"
- **Hint at more than meets the eye** before a fight — give players a chance to assess, prepare, or flee.
