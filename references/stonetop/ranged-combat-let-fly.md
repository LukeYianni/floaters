# Stonetop — Ranged Combat & Let Fly

## Let Fly

When you **calmly take an easy shot with a ranged weapon**, deal your damage — no roll required.

If **the shot is tricky or you're under pressure**, first roll +DEX:

**On a 10+**, you have a clear shot — deal your damage.

**On a 7–9**, pick 1:

- Deal your damage, but deplete your ammo (mark the next ammo status; skip if your weapon has no ammo track)
- Hold steady and wait for a clear shot; when the moment arrives (GM's call), deal your damage
- Move to get a clear shot — exposing yourself to danger or giving up some advantage (GM says how) — then deal your damage
- Rush the shot and deal your damage, leading to a cost or complication of the GM's choice

---

## Design notes

**The GM decides when a roll is needed.** An uncontested shot at a stationary target doesn't trigger the move. Let Fly only fires when there's real pressure: target is moving, cover is partial, you're being shot at yourself, time is against you.

**Partial success costs are positional or resource-based, not mutual.** Unlike Clash (melee), a 7–9 on Let Fly doesn't mean "you both take damage." The trade-off is one of:

1. **Resource depletion** (ammo — a future problem)
2. **Delayed payoff** (wait for the moment — you don't act now)
3. **Positional exposure** (you give up cover or advantage — a fictional problem)
4. **GM complication** (catch-all — could be anything)

This reflects the reality of ranged combat: getting shot at isn't the same as being hit. The danger at range is positional and situational, not a direct trade of harm.

**No hard list structure parallel to Clash.** Stonetop's Clash (melee) gives "deal damage and take their attack" as the default 7–9 result, with list options on 10+ for upgrades. Let Fly inverts this: on 7–9 you always deal damage, but pick one cost. On 10+, no cost at all.

---

## Relevance to Floaters / Hunt

Stonetop's model confirms that ranged combat's partial success cost should **not** be symmetric mutual damage. The Floaters Hunt move should encode this — the 4–5 result should have a different fictional structure than Fight's "trade blows."

Key question for Hunt: what is the *baseline cost* at 4–5, and what do the list options let you avoid or improve?
