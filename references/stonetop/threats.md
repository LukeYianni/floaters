# Stonetop: Threats

Source: GM Playbook (Threats section and Threat Moves reference sheet)

---

## What Threats Are

Threats are the lingering problems that cause trouble for the PCs, the steading, or the region — even the world. They are active forces with their own instincts, not passive obstacles. If the PCs don't intervene, threats advance on their own.

---

## When to Write Threats

- After the first session, based on what players revealed about their characters
- When Seasons Change results in a new threat and you choose to create one instead of making an existing threat worse
- When you introduced a monster, NPC, or thing that might cause trouble later
- When you expect the PCs to encounter the threat in an upcoming session

---

## Writing Up a Threat (8 Steps)

1. **Name and type** — give it a name and pick its type (see types below)
2. **Distance** — add it (and its type) to a threat tracker: Homefront, Nearby, or Distant
3. **Instinct** — how does it cause problems for others? Write it as "to ___" (e.g., "to enrich himself"). Give it one if it doesn't already have one.
4. **Description** — write a quick description, including related threats or NPCs
5. **Impending Doom** — if it has momentum, write its impending doom and 2–4 grim portents
6. **Stakes questions** — optional; open questions about how the threat will develop
7. **GM moves** — optional; pick 2–4 GM moves
8. **Custom player moves** — optional

---

## Impending Doom and Grim Portents

**Impending Doom** is the worst thing that happens if the threat goes unchecked — written as a single bad outcome.

**Grim Portents** are 2–4 checkboxes representing escalating stages toward the doom. Each is a concrete, observable event. They:
- track how close the doom is
- give the GM something to advance offscreen
- signal to players that the situation is worsening

---

## Distance: The Three Trackers

Threats are placed on one of three regional maps, tracking how close and present they are:

- **Homefront** — immediate; centered on the steading itself (the Ringwall, the Granary, the Great Wood edge)
- **Nearby** — upcoming; within a day or two of travel (the Foothills, the Flats, the Crossroads, the Ruined Tower)
- **Distant** — slow-burn; far off but coming (the Whitefang Mountains, the Steplands, the North and South Manmarch, Lygos and points south)

Distance is pacing. Distant threats advance slowly. Homefront threats demand immediate response. Threats can move closer or farther on the trackers between sessions.

---

## Updating Threats Between Sessions

Review each threat. If…

- it is no longer a threat → cross it off
- grim portents occurred → mark them
- the PCs foiled or changed its course → update its grim portents and impending doom appropriately
- it is moving toward something (but wasn't before) → write an impending doom and grim portents
- its instinct or threat type no longer ring true → revise them
- new stakes questions occur to you → write them down
- it got closer or farther away → move it from one threat tracker to the next

## The Eight Threat Types

### Affliction
A spreading harm — disease, corruption, social panic, a poisoned idea.

**Moves:**
- Worsen or quicken
- Spread to others / suck others in
- Mutate, take on a new form or aspect
- Eat away at something/someone
- Strip someone of honor/dignity
- Drive someone to desperation
- Justify selfishness, neglect
- Drive a wedge between people
- Cause delusion, stubbornness, foolishness
- Sow panic or despair
- Trigger shortages, hoarding
- Prompt violence, hatred, blame

---

### Beast
A creature or animal force acting on its nature.

**Moves:**
- Show up where it's not wanted
- Stalk or pursue prey
- Protect its home or family
- Make a show of strength, aggression
- Build or expand a nest/den/lair
- Modify its environment
- Flee or panic or rage
- Consume something (or someone)
- Grow or diminish in size or numbers

---

### Institution
A faction, organisation, or power structure pursuing its interests.

**Moves:**
- Sway public opinion
- Put someone in their place
- Change a rule, law, or custom
- Acquire leverage, resources, influence
- Denounce something or someone
- Support a course of action
- Recruit new members or minions
- Squabble amongst themselves
- Change leadership
- Negotiate a deal or treaty
- Send someone else to do their dirty work

---

### MacGuffin
An object, secret, or resource that causes trouble by existing — its absence, presence, or power draws others.

**Moves:**
- Reveal a secret
- Draw attention to itself
- Point to something else
- Generate envy/fear/discord
- Weigh heavily, become a burden
- Be the target of theft
- Go missing
- Perform its function, heedlessly
- Fail at the worst possible moment
- Leave its mark on someone or thing
- Become something greater, or lesser

---

### Magical Entity
A supernatural force, spirit, or ancient power with its own alien agenda.

**Moves:**
- Spy on someone, unseen/from afar
- Sense powerful longings/emotions
- Appear in glimpses, dreams, visions
- Offer service, secrets, power
- Demand an oath or sacrifice
- Lay a curse
- Twist a bargain to its favor
- Send forth minions to do its bidding
- Shape its environs, per its nature
- Pursue alien goals
- Foster rivalries with similar powers
- Grow or diminish in strength

---

### Rabble
A group, mob, or disorganised force that exerts pressure through sheer numbers or collective action.

**Moves:**
- Grow or gather in numbers
- Claim territory or resources
- Fall under a (new) leader's sway
- Undergo internal turmoil
- Make a show of strength/numbers
- Declare an enemy or an alliance
- Turn on one of their own
- Overwhelm a position or weaker group
- Despoil, loot, pillage, burn
- Refuse to be controlled/contained
- Disperse, scatter, flee

---

### Villain
An individual antagonist with intent, intelligence, and agency.

**Moves:**
- Grasp for power, gain followers or allies
- Find someone's weakness
- Make an offer, with strings attached
- Demand concessions, obedience, or respect
- Make threats, veiled or not
- Attack cautiously, holding reserves
- Attack ruthlessly, with little warning
- Reveal preparations made in advance
- Sacrifice another to advance a goal
- Betray an ally or a trust
- Take a prisoner
- Do the unthinkable

---

### Wildcard
An unpredictable force — a person, entity, or situation that doesn't fit neatly into the other categories.

**Moves:**
- Aggressively pursue their instinct
- Show their worth, or lack thereof
- Display the contents of their heart
- Provide advice/aid, wanted or not
- Reveal a secret, or keep one closely
- Draw attention to themselves/others
- Appear unannounced
- Act strangely (for them)
- Bear witness
- Tell stories (true or not)
- Make/keep/break/demand a promise
- Force an issue or a confrontation
- Stand resolute and refuse to budge

---

## Design Notes

- A threat's type determines which move list the GM draws from, but moves should feel true to the specific threat.
- The instinct drives what the threat does when the GM makes a move without a player trigger.
- Grim portents can be crossed off whether the PCs caused them or failed to prevent them — they mark what happened, not what the PCs did.
- Not every threat needs all eight fields. Small threats might just have a name, type, distance, instinct, and one grim portent.
- Threats can be related to each other — a Villain might be driving an Affliction, or a MacGuffin might be the cause of Rabble unrest. Note these connections in descriptions.
