# Stonetop — Weapons & Gear

## Inventory & Load

Every PC has an **Inventory insert**. Items printed on it are commonly available in Stonetop — any PC can access them without rolling.

Items marked ◇ or ◇◇ on the front of an item count against your **load**:

| Total ◇ marked | Load tier |
|---|---|
| up to 3 | **Light** — quick and quiet |
| 4–6 | **Normal** |
| 7–9 | **Heavy** — noisy, slow, hot, quick to tire |
| 10+ | Overloaded — GM calls consequences |

Small items (fit in a pocket, pouch, or boot with room to spare) do **not** contribute to load. Track them in the "Small items" section.

## Gear Terms & Tags

Tags in *italic typeface* are **fictional positioning** — they describe how something looks or behaves but have no specific mechanical effect. Tags in **regular typeface** are **mechanical**.

### Mechanical tags

| Tag | Effect |
|---|---|
| `[n] armor` | When you take damage, subtract *n*. Does **not** stack with other `[n] armor` tags. |
| `+1 armor` | Increases your armor value by 1. **Does** stack. |
| `area` | Affects everything in an area. |
| `awkward` | Unwieldy; requires space, gets stuck. |
| `crude` | Prone to break, wear out, or stop working. |
| `cumbersome` | Noisy, slow, hot, quick to tire — even without a heavy load (worse with one). |
| `+[n] damage` | Increases damage dealt by *n*. |
| `dangerous` | Causes trouble and collateral damage if you aren't careful (and maybe even if you are). |
| `forceful` | Can knock someone around, maybe off their feet. |
| `fragile` | Easy to break or ruin; pack it carefully. |
| `hours` | Lasts about an hour per ◯; mark ◯ to track time used. |
| `immobile` | Can't carry on your person; need a beast or vehicle. |
| `messy` | Particularly destructive damage — rips people and things apart. |
| `[n] piercing` | When you deal damage, ignore *n* points of the target's armor. For items listed as "x piercing," x = the steading's current Prosperity. |
| `reload` | After it's used, takes time/effort to reset before it can be used again. |
| `requires ___` | If you don't meet the requirements, it works poorly or not at all. |
| `slow` | Takes minutes or more to use; unlikely to be useful in a fight. |
| `thrown` | You can Let Fly with it (at *near* range). |
| `uses` | Mark ◯ each time you use it; gone/useless when all ◯ are marked. |
| `warm` | Keeps you warm in cold weather; uncomfortable and exhausting (and possibly dangerous) in hot weather. |

### Range tags

These indicate the distance within which a weapon or light source is effective.

| Tag | Distance |
|---|---|
| `band` | Tight quarters; up close and personal. |
| `close` | Melee range, 1–2 steps away. |
| `reach` | 3–4 steps away. |
| `near` | Up to ~30 steps away. |
| `far` | Up to ~100 steps, maybe more. |

## Ammo

Many ranged weapons track ammo with status bubbles:

**◯ plenty left → ◯ low ammo → ◯ all out**

When a move tells you to deplete your ammo (or the GM says so), mark the **next** status left to right. When marked **all out**, you can't use that weapon until you replenish ammunition.

---

## Damage & Armor Resolution

- Taking damage = losing HP.
- **Subtract the target's armor rating** from the damage dealt. This can reduce damage to 0 — they got hit, but HP (plot armor, luck, skill) absorbed it.
- `[n] piercing`: ignore *n* points of armor for **that attack only**. Armor value doesn't change for future attacks.
- "Ignores armor": armor doesn't apply at all — fire, psychic assault, etc.
- **Multiple combatants attacking at once**: roll the single highest damage die among them, then +1 per additional combatant. All tags from all attackers apply.
- Moves that say "lose [n] HP": armor **never** applies to this.

### Damage scale (GM guidance, when no prepared danger applies)

| Severity | Damage |
|---|---|
| Bruises, scrapes, light burns | d4 |
| Nasty flesh wounds, bruises | d8 |
| Broken bones, deep burns, debilitating pain | d8 |
| Death or dismemberment | d10 |

## Design patterns

These patterns are useful when designing Floaters weapons and tags:

- **Tags define fictional truth, not just modifiers.** `messy` doesn't just mean "more damage" — it means the GM should describe ripped flesh and broken things, and may use it to justify destroying shields or gear.
- **`forceful` changes positioning even without damage.** Knock targets around or off their feet regardless of HP outcome.
- **Piercing is tied to material quality.** Iron weapons at normal Prosperity have modest piercing. Better materials or forging = more piercing, expressed as a fixed number rather than x piercing.
- **Ammo tracks depletion narratively.** Three states (plenty / low / out) keep bookkeeping light while making resource pressure feel real.
- **Load slots (◇) are the cost of carrying power.** Two-slot weapons are meaningful trade-offs — they're heavier or bulkier, not just "better."
- **Range is relative, not metric.** `close`, `near`, `far` are GM-interpreted based on fictional positioning, not exact measurements.
